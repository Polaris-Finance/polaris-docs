## A

**Advanced**
The app area for expert, permissionless operations: PSM mint/redeem of pUSD and pGOLD, pETH-to-POLAR conversion, and the testnet faucet. Use it only after verifying the contract, function, token, amount, and quote; see [Advanced](/using-app/advanced).

**APR (Annual Percentage Rate)**
An annualized rate estimate, commonly shown for borrow cost or yield. APR is not a promised return: realized cost or yield can change with rates, activity, liquidations, fees, execution, and market conditions.

## B

**Bonding curve**
The two-way curve that mints and burns [pETH](/peth), giving it a contract-native buy/sell path with no liquidity providers. See [pETH Bonding Curve](/peth) and [pETH Floor Price](/peth/floor-price).

## C

**CDB (Collateralized Debt Branch)**
The aggregate protocol branch for one pAsset. A branch contains that pAsset's minting and redemption logic, debt accounting, risk parameters, Stability Pool, and all user [CDPs](#c). See [Core Concepts](/core-concepts).

**CDP (Collateralized Debt Position)**
Your individual borrowing position inside a [branch](#c): post pETH collateral, mint a pAsset directly from the protocol, and owe that debt back. In the Polaris app and these docs, an individual CDP is also called a [trove](#t). See [Minting pAssets](/minting).

**Conversion auction**
The mechanism that burns [pETH](/peth) to mint new [POLAR](/polar). See [Conversion Auctions](/polar/conversion-auctions).

**Converter rebate**
A stewardship-controlled percentage of ETH returned to the converter in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions). The dial between conversion volume and system yield.

## F

**Fee Router**
Receives protocol revenue, buffers it, and routes it to configured yield paths. See [Fee Router & Flows](/stewardship/fee-router).

**Floor Carry**
The possible return from holding [fpETH](#f) while the pETH [floor price](#f) rises. It is not a fixed APR or a guarantee; it depends on actual floor growth, market pricing, execution costs, and the redemption path remaining available.

**Floor price**
The contract-defined minimum price of [pETH](/peth), in ETH terms, under the bonding-curve assumptions. See [pETH Floor Price](/peth/floor-price).

**Floor ratio**
The floor price divided by the market price, as a percentage. It frames the curve-implied drawdown before gas, slippage, MEV, smart-contract risk, and chain-liveness risk. See [The Floor Price](/peth/floor-price).

**Flows**
The protocol's internal value movements (fees, conversions, yield distribution), routed by the [Fee Router](#f). Inward-facing flows sustain the core; outward-facing flows grow the ecosystem (see *Inward-facing / Outward-facing*).

**fpETH**
The floor-price leg created when [pETH](/peth/split) is split into fpETH and [vpETH](#v). fpETH can be redeemed for WETH at the pETH floor-price value under the splitter and bonding-curve assumptions, and it is used as reserve-loan collateral.

## G

**Guide**
The app area that routes users by goal, such as getting pETH, borrowing pAssets, earning yield, splitting pETH, or verifying safety. See [Polaris App](/using-app).

## I

**ICR (Individual Collateralization Ratio)**
The protocol-side inverse of [LTV](#l): pETH collateral value divided by pAsset debt. The app shows LTV; if ICR falls below the minimum, the equivalent LTV has crossed the maximum and the trove can be [liquidated](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

**Inward-facing / Outward-facing**
The design split between core mechanics handled by pETH/pAssets and ecosystem growth handled by [POLAR](/polar).

## L

**LTV (loan-to-value)**
The app-facing health metric for a [trove](#t): pAsset debt divided by pETH collateral value. Lower LTV is safer; if LTV exceeds the maximum, the trove can be [liquidated](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

**Liquidation**
The process that closes an undercollateralized [trove](#t): when its [LTV](#l) exceeds the maximum, the [Stability Pool](/yield) absorbs its debt and receives its collateral — or, if the pool is too small, the debt and collateral redistribute to other troves. See [Liquidations](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

## M

**Minting (mechanism)**
The inverse of [redemption](#r): when a pAsset trades *above* peg (e.g. pUSD > \$1), arbitrageurs add pETH to troves to mint the pAsset and sell it, pushing the price down. This restores the ceiling and is one of the onchain signals used to set interest rates. See [Minting pAssets](/minting).

**Minting (verb)**
Creating a pAsset by opening a [trove](#t) and drawing pAsset debt against pETH collateral. Polaris *mints* pAssets, not "issues" or "creates" them.

## O

**Oracle**
An onchain feed used to price ETH collateral. Peg awareness uses minting and redemption volume as internal signals; address status lives on [Contracts & Addresses](/resources/testnet#contracts-and-addresses).

## P

**pAsset**
A Polaris synthetic debt asset minted from a branch through user CDPs — pUSD, pGOLD, pCHF, and beyond. Each is overcollateralized by pETH and counterparty-free. pETH itself is the collateral token, not CDP-minted pAsset debt. See [The pAsset Catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).

**pBigMac**
A novelty pAsset demonstrating the "mint anything" thesis: if a price feed exists, Polaris can mint a pAsset that tracks it.

**pCHF**
The Polaris synthetic Swiss franc — a pAsset pegged to CHF.

**pETH**
ETH deposited into the two-way [bonding curve](/peth), and the only collateral Polaris accepts for pAsset troves. See [pETH Bonding Curve](/peth).

**pGOLD**
The Polaris synthetic gold pAsset, designed to track gold without issuer custody or KYC at the base protocol layer. See [The pAsset Catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).

**Pathfinders**
Early Polaris contributors and community members selected through public contribution paths.

**Polar Council**
A planned treasury-funded program for onboarding and ecosystem contributors. Terms are pending publication.

**POLAR**
The protocol's stewardship token and outward-facing growth lever. After the initial distribution, new POLAR can be minted *only* by burning pETH in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions). See [The POLAR Token](/polar).

**POLAR Staking**
The current app surface for staking POLAR and claiming routed rewards where exposed. It is distinct from forthcoming [vePOLAR](#v) locking.

**PSM (Peg Stability Module)**
The contract/module that handles pAsset minting and redemption mechanics for a pAsset branch. Use PSM or redemption actions only after checking the pAsset, fee, output amount, and contract address.

**pUSD**
The flagship pAsset, designed to track the US dollar and be backed by pETH collateral. See [Minting pAssets](/minting).

## R

**Recovery Mode**
A protective state triggered when a pAsset's system-wide reserve-to-debt ratio falls below a threshold. It restricts risk-increasing borrower actions; liquidation eligibility is unchanged. See [Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).

**Redemption**
Exchanging a pAsset for its underlying collateral at face value (minus a fee) when the pAsset trades *below* peg. Redemptions restore the floor and are one of the onchain signals used to set interest rates. See [Redemptions](/redemptions-liquidations).

**Reserve Loan**
A WETH loan against [fpETH](#f) floor-value collateral. It is separate from a pAsset trove: reserve loans use the pETH floor leg and reserve accounting, while troves mint pAssets against pETH collateral.

## S

**sepETH**
Sepolia ETH, the testnet-native gas asset used on Public Testnet 1. It has no mainnet ETH value; when wrapped for app flows it may appear as WETH.

**SP (Stability Pool)**
Common abbreviation for the [Stability Pool](#s), the pAsset first-loss pool that absorbs liquidations and pays depositors borrower-interest share and liquidation gains.

**Split**
The app flow and contract operation that turns pETH into equal amounts of [fpETH](#f) and [vpETH](#v), or merges equal fpETH and vpETH amounts back into pETH. See [pETH Split](/peth/split).

**Spike-and-decay**
The pricing model for [conversion auctions](/polar/conversion-auctions): the conversion price continuously decays until a conversion *spikes* it upward, proportionally to the POLAR supply that conversion inflated.

**Stability Pool**
A reserve of pAssets that absorbs the debt of [liquidated](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations) troves and is the protocol's primary venue for earning yield. See [The Stability Pool](/yield).

**StablecoinOS**
The branch factory: many distinct pAsset branches, each issuing its own pAsset, all sharing one pETH collateral and bonding curve. See [Core Concepts](/core-concepts).

**Stewardship**
The bounded role stewards play: tuning *quantitative parameters within the bounds the contracts define*. The immutable core cannot be changed; stewards steward, they do not govern. See [Stewardship, not Governance](/stewardship).

## T

**TCR (Total Collateralization Ratio)**
The system-wide reserve-to-debt health metric for a pAsset. Dropping below the configured threshold triggers [Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).

**Trove**
The Polaris app term for an individual [CDP](#c). Each trove is isolated, with its own collateral, debt, and [LTV](#l). Polaris uses *trove*, never "vault". See [Minting pAssets](/minting).

## V

**vePOLAR**
The forthcoming vote-escrow layer for POLAR, used for [stewardship](/stewardship) rights and a yield path. See [vePOLAR](/stewardship/vepolar).

**vpETH**
The volatility-premium leg created when [pETH](/peth/split) is split into [fpETH](#f) and vpETH. vpETH represents exposure to pETH's market value above the floor and can lose significant value if the premium compresses.

## W

**WETH**
Wrapped ETH, the ERC-20 form of ETH used by app contracts and testnet flows. On Sepolia, WETH is a test token backed by testnet ETH, not mainnet ETH.

## Z

**Zap**
An app flow that bundles multiple steps, such as WETH -> pUSD -> Stability Pool deposit. A zap is an execution shortcut, not a risk reducer; check approvals, simulation, slippage, quote freshness, and first-loss risk. See [Zap](/using-app/zap).
