# Polaris Glossary

Every key Polaris term, alphabetized. Each entry is one or two sentences and links to its full treatment where one exists. For the narrative map of how the pieces fit, read [Core Concepts](/core-concepts).

## A

**Advanced**
The app area for manual or lower-level tools such as approvals, PSM/redemption actions, reserve loans, conversion, and POLAR Staking controls. Use it only after verifying the contract, function, token, amount, and quote; see [Advanced](/using-app/advanced).

**APR (Annual Percentage Rate)**
An annualized rate estimate, commonly shown for borrow cost or yield. APR is not a promised return: realized cost or yield can change with rates, activity, liquidations, fees, execution, and market conditions.

## B

**Bonding curve**
The two-way curve that backs [pETH](/peth/bonding-curve). It enforces a mathematical relationship between pETH supply and price (minting raises it, burning lowers it), providing a contract-native buy/sell path with no liquidity providers and a [floor price](/peth/floor-price) designed to rise under the curve invariants. See [The Bonding Curve](/peth/bonding-curve).

## C

**CDP (Collateralized Debt Position)**
The mechanism behind minting: post collateral, mint a pAsset directly from the protocol rather than borrowing another user's funds. Each position is an isolated [trove](#t). See [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets).

**Conversion auction**
The mechanism connecting pETH and POLAR: it burns [pETH](/peth/bonding-curve) to mint new [POLAR](/polar/polar-token). The most protocol-synergetic action possible: every conversion raises the floor and releases ETH yield. See [Conversion Auctions](/polar/conversion-auctions).

**Converter rebate**
A stewardship-controlled percentage of ETH returned to the converter in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions). The dial between conversion volume and system yield.

## F

**Fee Router**
Receives pETH from conversions and bonding-curve swap-fee buybacks, buffers it over a reward period, and distributes pETH rewards to a stewardship-designated receiver — currently troves, pro-rata to debt. Staker yield is distributed separately, in pAsset terms, via the interest router. See [Stewardship](/stewardship/stewardship).

**Floor Carry**
The possible return from holding [fpETH](#f) while the pETH [floor price](#f) rises. It is not a fixed APR or a guarantee; it depends on actual floor growth, market pricing, execution costs, and the redemption path remaining available.

**Floor price**
The contract-defined minimum price of [pETH](/peth/bonding-curve), in ETH terms, under the bonding-curve invariants. It is designed to move upward during normal protocol operation and represents the modeled *last person out* price if everyone exited at once, subject to contract correctness, Ethereum liveness, gas, slippage, and MEV. See [The Floor Price](/peth/floor-price).

**Floor ratio**
The floor price divided by the market price, as a percentage. It frames the curve-implied drawdown before gas, slippage, MEV, smart-contract risk, and chain-liveness risk. See [The Floor Price](/peth/floor-price).

**Flows**
The protocol's internal value movements (fees, conversions, yield distribution), routed by the [Fee Router](#f). Inward-facing flows sustain the core; outward-facing flows grow the ecosystem (see *Inward-facing / Outward-facing*).

**fpETH**
The floor-price leg created when [pETH](/peth/split) is split into fpETH and [vpETH](#v). fpETH can be redeemed for WETH at the pETH floor-price value under the splitter and bonding-curve assumptions, and it is used as reserve-loan collateral.

## G

**Guide**
The app area that routes users by goal, such as getting pETH, borrowing pAssets, earning yield, splitting pETH, or verifying safety. See [Guide](/using-app/guide).

## I

**ICR (Individual Collateralization Ratio)**
The collateralization ratio of a single [trove](#t): the value of its pETH collateral divided by its pAsset debt. If a trove's ICR falls below the minimum, it can be [liquidated](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

**Inward-facing / Outward-facing**
The design split deciding which token handles what. *Inward-facing* flows (interest rates, floor growth, Stability Pool incentivization, liquidations) are handled by pETH and the pAssets. *Outward-facing* growth (external liquidity, Flow onboarding and weights, ecosystem incentives) is handled by [POLAR](/polar/polar-token).

## L

**Liquidation**
The process that closes an undercollateralized [trove](#t): when its [ICR](#i) drops below the minimum, the [Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool) absorbs its debt and receives its collateral. See [Liquidations](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

## M

**Minting (mechanism)**
The inverse of [redemption](#r): when a pAsset trades *above* peg (e.g. pUSD > \$1), arbitrageurs add pETH to troves to mint the pAsset and sell it, pushing the price down. This restores the ceiling and is one of the onchain signals used to set interest rates. See [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets).

**Minting (verb)**
Creating a pAsset by opening a [trove](#t) and drawing pAsset debt against pETH collateral. Polaris *mints* pAssets, not "issues" or "creates" them.

## O

**Oracle**
An onchain feed Polaris is expected to use to price ETH collateral. Peg awareness needs no oracle: redemption and minting volume serve as an internal price signal for each pAsset. The final provider and feed address are forthcoming; Testnet 2 uses Sepolia and production is intended for Ethereum mainnet. See [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets), [Launch Status](/launch-status), and [Contracts & Addresses](/resources/contracts).

## P

**pAsset**
A Polaris synthetic debt asset minted from a CDP — pUSD, pGOLD, pCHF, and beyond. Each is overcollateralized by pETH and counterparty-free. pETH itself is the collateral token, not CDP-minted pAsset debt. See [The pAsset Catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).

**pBigMac**
A novelty pAsset demonstrating the "mint anything" thesis: if a price feed exists, Polaris can mint a pAsset that tracks it.

**pCHF**
The Polaris synthetic Swiss franc — a pAsset pegged to CHF.

**pETH**
ETH deposited into the two-way [bonding curve](/peth/bonding-curve). The *only* collateral Polaris accepts: yield-bearing, with a [floor price](/peth/floor-price) designed to rise under the curve invariants. Volatile against ETH, with a contract-defined floor under stated assumptions. See [The Bonding Curve](/peth/bonding-curve).

**pGOLD**
The Polaris synthetic gold pAsset, designed to track gold without issuer custody or KYC at the base protocol layer. See [The pAsset Catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).

**Pathfinders**
Core believers, not just early followers. Access is earned through merit (explanatory threads, deep research, finding Easter eggs) and rewarded with early alphas, testnet access, and dev-stage updates.

**Polar Council**
A planned treasury-funded program for onboarding and ecosystem contributors, similar in spirit to veAERO's Flight School. Its final allocation, eligibility, operating framework, and reporting rules are pending publication.

**POLAR**
The protocol's stewardship token and outward-facing growth lever. After the initial distribution, new POLAR can be minted *only* by burning pETH in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions). See [The POLAR Token](/polar/polar-token).

**POLAR Staking**
The current app staking surface for staking POLAR and claiming routed rewards where the deployment exposes it. It is distinct from forthcoming [vePOLAR](#v) locking, which adds lock durations, vote-power decay, and stewardship rights.

**PolarEX**
Polaris' integrated DEX. Its swap fees are one of the system's yield sources.

**PSM (Peg Stability Module)**
The contract/module that handles pAsset minting and redemption mechanics for a pAsset instance. Use PSM or redemption actions only after checking the pAsset, fee, output amount, and contract address.

**pUSD**
The flagship pAsset, designed to track the US dollar and be backed by pETH collateral. Intended to be counterparty-free and fully onchain. See [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets).

## R

**Recovery Mode**
A protocol-wide protective state (Emergency Mode in the contracts) triggered when a pAsset's system-wide reserve-to-debt ratio (the [TCR](#t)) falls below a fixed threshold. While active, operations that reduce a trove's collateral or increase its debt are restricted (they must clear a higher minimum ratio and may not lower an existing trove's ICR); liquidation eligibility is unchanged. See [Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).

**Redemption**
Exchanging a pAsset for its underlying collateral at face value (minus a fee) when the pAsset trades *below* peg. Redemptions restore the floor and are one of the onchain signals used to set interest rates. See [Redemptions](/redemptions-liquidations/redemptions).

**Reserve Loan**
A WETH loan against [fpETH](#f) floor-value collateral. It is separate from a pAsset trove: reserve loans use the pETH floor leg and reserve accounting, while troves mint pAssets against pETH collateral.

## S

**sepETH**
Sepolia ETH, the testnet-native gas asset used on Testnet 2. It has no mainnet ETH value; when wrapped for app flows it may appear as WETH.

**SP (Stability Pool)**
Common abbreviation for the [Stability Pool](#s), the pAsset first-loss pool that absorbs liquidations and pays depositors borrower-interest share and liquidation gains.

**Split**
The app flow and contract operation that turns pETH into equal amounts of [fpETH](#f) and [vpETH](#v), or merges equal fpETH and vpETH amounts back into pETH. See [pETH Split](/peth/split).

**Spike-and-decay**
The pricing model for [conversion auctions](/polar/conversion-auctions): the conversion price continuously decays until a conversion *spikes* it upward, proportionally to the POLAR supply that conversion inflated.

**Stability Pool**
A reserve of pAssets that absorbs the debt of [liquidated](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations) troves and is the protocol's primary venue for earning yield. See [The Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool).

**StablecoinOS**
The CDP-factory layer: many distinct pAssets, each issued by its own CDP instance, all sharing one pETH collateral and bonding curve. See [Core Concepts](/core-concepts).

**Stewardship**
The bounded role stewards play: tuning *quantitative parameters within the bounds the contracts define*. The immutable core cannot be changed; stewards steward, they do not govern. See [Stewardship, not Governance](/stewardship/stewardship).

## T

**TCR (Total Collateralization Ratio)**
The system-wide health metric: the bonding-curve reserve backing total collateral, valued in the pAsset, divided by total pAsset debt (the contract's reserve-to-debt ratio). Not a plain market-value-of-collateral over debt figure. Dropping below the configured threshold triggers [Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).

**Trove**
An individual borrowing position in a Polaris CDP. Each trove is isolated, with its own collateral, debt, and [ICR](#i). Polaris uses *trove*, never "vault" or "position". See [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets).

## V

**vePOLAR**
The forthcoming vote-escrow layer for POLAR — POLAR locked for [stewardship](/stewardship/stewardship) rights and a share of protocol yield. It is distinct from current POLAR Staking; the vePOLAR locking mechanism, contract address, lock durations, and vote-power rules are pending audited deployment. See [vePOLAR](/stewardship/vepolar).

**vpETH**
The volatility-premium leg created when [pETH](/peth/split) is split into [fpETH](#f) and vpETH. vpETH represents exposure to pETH's market value above the floor and can lose significant value if the premium compresses.

## W

**WETH**
Wrapped ETH, the ERC-20 form of ETH used by app contracts and testnet flows. On Sepolia, WETH is a test token backed by testnet ETH, not mainnet ETH.

## Z

**Zap**
An app flow that bundles multiple steps, such as WETH -> pUSD or pGOLD -> Stability Pool deposit. A zap is an execution shortcut, not a risk reducer; check approvals, simulation, slippage, quote freshness, and first-loss risk. See [Zap](/using-app/zap).
