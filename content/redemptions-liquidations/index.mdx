# Peg Defense: Issuance And Redemptions

Polaris uses two permissionless mechanisms to help pAssets trade around their reference value.

When a pAsset trades above peg, new issuance can expand supply.

When a pAsset trades below peg, redemptions can contract supply.

Together, these mechanisms create arbitrage loops that push pAssets back toward peg without relying on an offchain issuer or discretionary intervention.

## When pAssets Trade Above Peg

If USDp trades above $1, an arbitrageur can create new USDp against pETH collateral and sell it into the market.

That increases USDp supply and applies downward pressure on price.

In simple terms:
1. USDp trades above peg.
2. An arbitrageur adds pETH collateral and mints USDp.
3. The arbitrageur sells USDp into the market to capture the spread.
4. Supply expands until the arbitrage opportunity closes.

## When pAssets Trade Below Peg

If USDp trades below $1, an arbitrageur can buy USDp cheaply and redeem it for pETH collateral at the protocol reference value, minus fees and execution costs.

That buys USDp from the market and reduces supply.

In simple terms:
1. USDp trades below peg.
2. An arbitrageur buys discounted USDp.
3. The arbitrageur redeems it for pETH collateral.
4. Supply contracts until the opportunity closes.

## How Positions Are Affected

Issuance and redemptions are socialized across all open positions for the affected pAsset.

No position is targeted before another. The protocol does not redeem against the riskiest position first, and a low LTV does not exempt a position from redemption exposure.

New issuance adds collateral and debt across open positions. Redemptions reduce collateral and debt across open positions.

Redemptions are not liquidations. If your position is redeemed against, your debt and collateral can both shrink. Your LTV may improve, but your position becomes smaller and your pETH exposure falls.

Issuance and redemption fees also flow to the affected positions. Unlike designs where these fees are routed to a separate stakeholder group, Polaris directs spike-and-decay fees back to the positions impacted by the mechanism.

## Why This Matters

Polaris does not rely on an offchain issuer to defend pAsset pegs.

Above-peg pressure invites issuance.

Below-peg pressure invites redemptions.

Both mechanisms are permissionless and driven by arbitrage. The protocol uses this activity as a peg signal for autonomous interest rates.

## What To Watch

- Fees, gas, slippage, and MEV affect whether arbitrage is profitable.
- Issuance can increase the size of open positions by adding debt and collateral.
- Redemptions can shrink open positions even when they are solvent.
- Lower LTV protects against liquidation, not redemption.
- Issuance and redemption activity can change system-wide debt and collateral.
