# Polaris Liquidations

## What a liquidation is

A **liquidation** closes a position whose **LTV exceeds the maximum** - the point at which its collateral no longer comfortably covers its debt. The protocol cancels the position's debt and seizes its pETH collateral.

The purpose is solvency. Every pAsset is a claim on collateral; leaving undercollateralized positions open would leave the pAsset not fully backed. Liquidations are how Polaris is designed to keep backing intact, subject to functioning contracts, live Ethereum execution, available gas, valid pricing data, keeper/executor availability, and audited invariants.

Unlike a [redemption](/redemptions-liquidations), a liquidation is not an arbitrage you can opt into at par - it is a forced close triggered by your own LTV crossing a hard line.

## Where the debt and collateral go

Liquidation runs in two tiers.

1. **The Earn Vault absorbs it first.** The [Earn Vault](/yield) holds a reserve of the pAsset. When a position is liquidated, the vault burns pAsset equal to the position's debt and receives pETH collateral covering the debt plus the liquidation penalty (any surplus goes back to the borrower - see below). Because liquidated positions are typically seized while still *overcollateralized* but past the LTV liquidation line, the vault usually receives more value in pETH than the pAsset it burns. That surplus is the depositors' compensation.

2. **Redistribution is the fallback.** If the Earn Vault is empty or too small to cover the debt, the position's debt *and* collateral spread across the remaining positions on that pAsset, in proportion to their size. Other borrowers take on a slice of debt and a matching slice of pETH. The system stays solvent either way.

| Path | Trigger | Absorbs debt | Receives collateral |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Earn Vault** | Vault has sufficient pAsset | Vault burns its pAsset | Depositors, based on their share |
| **Redistribution** | Vault empty or insufficient | Other positions take it on | Other positions, based on size |

**Collateral surplus.** When the Earn Vault absorbs a liquidation, the position surrenders only what covers its debt, a liquidation penalty, and gas compensation for the liquidator. Any collateral beyond that goes to the **Collateral Surplus Pool**, where the former borrower can claim it. The claim is an explicit transaction - the surplus is not pushed back automatically, and it waits in the pool until claimed. (Collateral spread through the redistribution fallback is not returned this way.)

## Values to check

Current MCR, Recovery Mode, fee, and liquidation values live on [Parameters](/resources/testnet#parameters). This page explains the closeout path; the parameter page owns the numbers.

Before acting, check:

- The pAsset's max LTV or inverse minimum collateral ratio.
- Any liquidation penalty, gas compensation, and keeper incentive.
- Earn Vault coverage and whether redistribution can apply.
- Oracle freshness and fallback behavior.

## Worked example: liquidation

The MCR number below is the current Public Testnet 1 pUSD/pGOLD value as of June 8, 2026. Parameters publish this as a 115% minimum collateral ratio, equivalent to about 87.0% max LTV. A borrower has 10,000 pUSD of debt and pETH collateral valued at \$11,300, so the LTV is about 88.5% - above the current testnet maximum, and liquidatable.

If the Earn Vault is large enough, it burns 10,000 pUSD and receives the position's pETH. The borrower's debt is cleared; collateral covers the debt plus the contract-defined liquidation penalty and gas compensation where available, with any surplus returned for the borrower to claim. Depositors receive pETH proportionally; in a normal overcollateralized liquidation, that pETH should be worth more than the pUSD burned, but the exact gain depends on the penalty, execution timing, pETH price, gas, and slippage.

If the Earn Vault is empty or too small, the uncovered portion follows the redistribution path: remaining positions inherit a proportional slice of both the liquidated debt and collateral.

## Risks

<Callout variant="danger" title="Warning - For Borrowers">
Liquidation is involuntary and can happen fast. A liquidated position gives up its collateral to cover the debt plus a liquidation penalty and gas compensation; any surplus is returned to you to claim, but the penalty makes liquidation a real loss to avoid. Keep a <strong>buffer</strong> below max LTV so a sharp move in collateral price does not cross the line. The thinner your margin, the less room you have when volatility hits. See <a href="/minting/managing-your-trove">Managing Your Position</a>.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="danger" title="Warning - For Earn Vault Depositors">
You are the system's first-loss backstop. In a flash crash, positions can be liquidated near - or in extreme cases below - 100% collateralization, and you may receive pETH worth less than the pAsset burned. Over a full cycle the surplus from healthy liquidations compensates for that risk, but it is not riskless yield. See <a href="/yield">The Earn Vault</a> and the <a href="/resources/risk-disclosure">Risk Disclosure</a>.
</Callout>

## How liquidation interacts with system health

A position's LTV is not all that matters. When system-wide backing runs thin, the protocol enters [Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode), which tightens risk-increasing borrower operations while it lasts. Liquidation eligibility is unchanged: a position is liquidated whenever its LTV exceeds the maximum, in every mode. Recovery Mode is the system-level safeguard complementing the position-level liquidation described here.
