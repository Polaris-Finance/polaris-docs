# Polaris Recovery Mode

## The Total Collateralization Ratio

The **Total Collateralization Ratio (TCR)** is the system-wide health metric for a pAsset. It is not a simple market-value-of-collateral over debt figure: it measures the bonding-curve reserve backing all the pETH collateral — the collateral scaled by the curve's reserve-backing fraction and valued at the reserve (redemption) price — divided by total pAsset debt outstanding.

$$
\text{TCR} = \frac{\text{Reserve backing the collateral} \times \text{reserve price}}{\text{Total debt}}
$$

A high TCR means a deep, well-backed system; a low TCR means the reserve behind that pAsset is thin relative to its debt. In the contracts this is the reserve-to-debt ratio, and crossing the threshold triggers the protective state (internally called Emergency Mode).

## What Recovery Mode is

**Recovery Mode** is a protective state the protocol enters automatically when the TCR drops below a set threshold. It is not a freeze and not an emergency shutdown; it tightens the rules to make the system **harder to destabilize** while collateral is scarce.

The purpose is system-level solvency. When the TCR is low, a single sharp move could tip the whole pAsset undercollateralized. Recovery Mode raises the bar for risky behavior so the system pulls back toward health instead of sliding further.

## Parameters

Recovery Mode thresholds and current per-pAsset values live on [Parameters](/resources/parameters). This page explains how behavior changes when the protective state is active.

## How behavior changes

Recovery Mode does **not** change which troves are liquidatable. In every mode, a trove is liquidatable only once its ICR falls below the minimum (the MCR). What tightens is the set of **borrower actions** allowed while the protective state is active.

| Your action | Normal Mode | Recovery Mode |
|---|---|---|
| **Add collateral / repay debt** | Allowed | Allowed |
| **Withdraw collateral / mint more debt** | Allowed if ICR stays above the minimum | Allowed only if the resulting ICR clears a higher recovery minimum and does not decrease your ICR |
| **Liquidation eligibility** | ICR below the minimum | ICR below the minimum (unchanged) |

The principle: Recovery Mode restricts actions that would make the system thinner, while always allowing actions that strengthen it. Liquidation eligibility stays exactly where it is in Normal Mode; only risk-increasing operations change, now clearing a higher bar and never lowering your ICR. The net effect pushes every participant toward adding collateral and trimming debt — exactly what raises the system's backing above the threshold and exits Recovery Mode.

> **Note:** Current Public Testnet 1 values are published on [Parameters](/resources/parameters): the Recovery Mode trigger is 110% and the recovery-mode action minimum is 150% for pUSD and pGOLD. Production values remain pending.

## Worked example: Recovery Mode

The numbers below are illustrative only. The trigger is the system's reserve-backing-to-debt ratio, so a plain collateral-value over debt division is only a rough proxy; treat this as shape, not arithmetic.

Suppose the protective state is active for pUSD. A trove at 130% ICR is **not** liquidated by Recovery Mode: liquidation eligibility is unchanged, so any trove above the normal minimum stays safe in every mode. What changes is the borrower's freedom to act. On the current Public Testnet 1 values, that 130% trove is above the 115% MCR but below the 150% recovery-mode action minimum, so it cannot withdraw collateral or mint more debt unless the resulting ICR clears that higher bar and does not lower its ICR. Adding collateral and repaying debt are always allowed.

The borrower's defense is the same as always: add collateral, repay debt, or both. For example, a trove with \$13,000 of collateral and 10,000 pUSD of debt is at 130%; repaying 2,000 pUSD moves it to \$13,000 / 8,000 pUSD, or 162.5%, before fees and price movement — and a healthier ICR also widens your room to act while the system is stressed.

## How to make your trove safe

If the system is in or near Recovery Mode, the move is the same one that always protects a trove: raise your **ICR**.

1. **Add pETH collateral.** More collateral against the same debt lifts your ICR directly.
2. **Repay debt.** Burning pAsset to reduce what you owe lifts your ICR from the other side.
3. **Do both early.** Recovery Mode restricts risk-increasing actions and hurts most when you are forced to react under stress, so build the buffer *before* stress arrives, not during it.

See [Managing Your Trove](/minting/managing-your-trove) for the mechanics, and [Liquidations](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations) for what happens to a trove that falls below the line.

> **Warning:** Recovery Mode is most likely to bite during exactly the volatile conditions when adding collateral is hardest. The only reliable defense is a wide buffer maintained in calm markets. See the [Risk Disclosure](/resources/risk-disclosure).
