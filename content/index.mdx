# Polaris Finance Documentation

<PageStatusBanner lastUpdated="June 8, 2026" />

Polaris is a yield layer for DeFi built around **pETH** — purpose-built collateral for Polaris branches and CDPs that is also a yield-bearing asset in its own right.

- **pETH**, backed by the bonding curve: a foundational yield primitive with strong constant liquidity
- **pUSD**, **pGOLD**, and other Polaris immutable stablecoins (**pAssets**) use **pETH** as collateral
- **pETH** can be split into **fpETH** for protected yield exposure and **vpETH** for maximal upside with no downside protection
- The core protocol is immutable; bounded parameters will be stewarded by **vePOLAR** (forthcoming)

Polaris is trustless, immutable infrastructure for minting pAssets backed entirely by onchain collateral and yield — competitive risk-adjusted yield for both ETH and stablecoins. [Flows](/stewardship/fee-router) let third-party protocols earn a share of Polaris revenue by building on its assets.

This documentation covers how the system works, how to use the app, and which risks to check before signing. For current network, contract, and actionability status, start with [Launch Status](/launch-status) — or jump straight to the [Public Testnet Quickstart](/quickstart).

## The three engines

Polaris has three engines:

| Engine | What it is | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **1.&nbsp;pETH** | ETH deposited into a two-way **bonding curve**, with a contract-defined floor under stated assumptions | A capital-efficient, yield-bearing collateral with a contract-defined exit path |
| **2.&nbsp;pAssets** | Synthetic pegged (USD, Gold, etc.) assets minted against collateral, through pAsset branches and user CDPs | Counterparty-free stables that scale with onchain activity |
| **3.&nbsp;POLAR** | The stewardship token, minted only by burning pETH in a **conversion auction** | Aligns long-term incentives and steers the protocol within hardcoded bounds |
