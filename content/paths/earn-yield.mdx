# Earn Yield on Polaris

**Who this is for:** holders who want protocol-native yield from pAssets, pETH, current POLAR Staking, or future locked POLAR — without token emissions, bribes, or offchain backing.

**Prerequisites:** a pAsset to deposit (so most readers come here after [Borrow pAssets](/paths/borrow-passets)) or pETH, plus awareness that Stability Pool yield carries liquidation-conversion exposure to your principal.

> **Launch status:** Yield flows are conceptual and testnet-actionable only where Sepolia artifacts exist. Production deposits are not mainnet-actionable until [Polaris Launch Status](/launch-status) publishes the official app, production pool addresses, final splits, buffering parameters, and audit status.

## Reading sequence

Read these in order — start with where the yield originates before committing capital.

1. [Where the Yield Comes From](/yield/yield-sources) — the full source map, so you can judge whether each yield stream is sustainable rather than subsidized.
2. [The Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool) — the primary venue for pAsset yield, and how liquidations can convert your deposit into discounted pETH.
3. [Deposit to the Stability Pool](/yield/deposit-to-stability-pool) — the user flow you will follow once the app is public.
4. [The Fee Router](/stewardship/fee-router) — how pETH from fees and conversions reaches configured receivers, and how that differs from pAsset-denominated staking rewards.
5. [Flows](/stewardship/flows) — only if you are an integrator or operator seeking protocol revenue sharing; safe to skip as a plain depositor.

## What yield is not

Polaris yield is not a token-emission program, gauge market, bribe market, T-bill wrapper, or CEX lending strategy. It comes from protocol activity: borrower interest, liquidation gains, bonding-curve fees, conversion gains, and other onchain fees described in the yield docs.

## What to verify at launch

- The official app URL.
- The Stability Pool address for the pAsset you deposit.
- The final interest split and liquidation parameters for that pAsset.
- The Fee Router address and verified source.
- Whether any wrapper vault you use is official, audited, and listed by the project that operates it.
