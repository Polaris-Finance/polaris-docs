# Hold or Use Polaris pETH

**Who this is for:** anyone who wants to hold pETH for its rising floor, use it as collateral, or understand the asset before borrowing against it.

**Prerequisites:** a wallet and the right ETH for the environment: Sepolia ETH for Public Testnet 1, or ETH on Ethereum mainnet after production launch. No trove or pAsset is required to hold pETH; borrowing is optional and comes later.

> **Launch status:** pETH is testnet-actionable only through the current Sepolia deployment manifests. Production pETH use is not mainnet-actionable until [Polaris Launch Status](/launch-status) publishes the official app, production bonding-curve address, verified source, final curve parameters, and audit status. Do not trust unofficial addresses.

## Reading sequence

Read these in order to understand pETH and its downside bound before relying on it.

1. [The Bonding Curve](/peth/bonding-curve) — how ETH deposits become pETH, and where your exit liquidity comes from.
2. [The Floor Price](/peth/floor-price) — the contract-defined downside bound in ETH terms under the curve assumptions.
3. [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets) — only if you may use pETH inside a trove; explains what changes when pETH becomes collateral.
4. [Where the Yield Comes From](/yield/yield-sources) — how bonding-curve and conversion activity raises the floor for pETH holders.
5. [Risk Disclosure](/resources/risk-disclosure) — read before using pETH as collateral anywhere, inside or outside Polaris.

## What to verify at launch

- The official pETH contract address and verified source.
- The bonding-curve address on the intended network: Sepolia for Public Testnet 1, Ethereum mainnet after production launch.
- Final curve, fee, and floor-ratio parameters once published.
- The exit path from pETH back to ETH.
- Any external venue's risk controls before using pETH outside Polaris.
