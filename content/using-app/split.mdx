# Split

<PageStatusBanner lastUpdated="June 9, 2026" />

## What it does

Split converts pETH into matching fpETH and vpETH at a fixed **1:1:1** ratio — no fees, no oracle, and fully reversible: merge 1 fpETH + 1 vpETH back into 1 pETH at any time. **fpETH** is the floor leg, redeemable for the bonding curve's floor price (which only ever rises), so it gains in ETH terms with little volatility. **vpETH** is the above-floor leg, absorbing all of pETH's volatility above the floor. Use this page for the workflow; use [pETH Split](/peth/split) for the mechanism.

## Before you start

- Start from the [Dashboard](/using-app) and verify the current app and network first.
- Hold pETH for splitting, or equal fpETH and vpETH amounts for merging.
- Confirm which leg you intend to hold after the action — they behave very differently.

## Steps/checks

1. Choose Split or Merge.
2. Enter the amount and review the expected fpETH + vpETH (or pETH) output.
3. Approve pETH, fpETH, or vpETH if required.
4. Sign the transaction.
5. Confirm wallet balances update.

A split worked when pETH decreases and fpETH plus vpETH each increase by the same amount. A merge worked when equal fpETH and vpETH decrease and pETH increases.

## Common failures

- Merge amount exceeds the smaller of your fpETH or vpETH balances.
- Token balance exists but is hidden in the wallet UI.
- Approval was granted for one leg but not the other.
- You expected holding one leg to behave like holding unsplit pETH.

## Risks/links

Holding only fpETH or only vpETH changes your exposure: fpETH tracks the floor with little volatility, while vpETH amplifies upside and downside. Read [pETH Split](/peth/split), [pETH Floor Price](/peth/floor-price), and [Risk Disclosure](/resources/risk-disclosure) before relying on either leg.
