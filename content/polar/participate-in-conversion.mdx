# Participate in a POLAR Conversion

This is the how-to. For the mechanism behind it, read [Conversion Auctions](/polar/conversion-auctions); for POLAR's role, read [The POLAR Token](/polar/polar-token).

> **Note:**
> Conversions are a continuous, permissionless auction tuned for [arbitrage bots and MEV searchers](/polar/conversion-auctions). Converting profitably means competing with them. Most users will hold POLAR, use current POLAR Staking where the app exposes it, or wait for [vePOLAR](/stewardship/vepolar) locking instead of converting directly.

> **Actionability:**
> Conversions are testnet-actionable only on Sepolia where the current deployment manifests expose the flow. Production conversions are not mainnet-actionable until [Polaris Launch Status](/launch-status) publishes the official app, production conversion contract, final parameters, quote fields, transaction preview, and audit status.

## Before you sign

Conversion is **one-way**: the pETH you burn is gone for good and POLAR cannot be converted back. Your proceeds depend on auction-price and [rebate](/polar/conversion-auctions#the-converter-rebate) dynamics that shift between preview and inclusion, and you compete with bots for the same spread. Convert only on the live previewed quote, never on the examples here. See the [Risk Disclosure](/resources/risk-disclosure).

Do not attempt a conversion until all of these are true:

- The official app URL is published on [Polaris Launch Status](/launch-status).
- The conversion contract is listed on [Contracts & Addresses](/resources/contracts) for the intended network: Sepolia (`11155111`) for Public Testnet 1, or Ethereum mainnet (`1`) after production launch.
- The pETH amount, POLAR output, ETH rebate, fee, slippage, and quote timestamp are visible.
- The conversion price, POLAR market price, rebate, and price-spike preview are visible in the app before signing.
- Any pETH approval points to the expected conversion contract and amount.
- You are comfortable with a one-way burn: pETH converted to POLAR cannot be converted back.

## What you need

- **pETH** to burn — acquired by buying on the [bonding curve](/peth/bonding-curve) or on a DEX.
- A wallet connected to the Polaris app.

## Steps

<Steps>

### Open the conversion panel

It shows the live **conversion price** and POLAR's **market price** side by side.

### Watch the decaying price

The conversion price decays continuously and spikes upward each time someone converts. A conversion is profitable when it drifts **below** POLAR's market price; that spread is your margin.

### Read the quote

Enter a pETH amount. The panel previews the **POLAR you will receive**, the **ETH rebate** at the current [rebate](/polar/conversion-auctions) setting, and the resulting **price spike**. Because the spike scales with the fraction of POLAR supply minted, larger amounts price progressively worse; favor smaller increments.

### Decide

Convert only while the previewed POLAR (plus rebate) beats acquiring POLAR on the open market. If the spread has closed, wait for decay to reopen it.

### Burn pETH ⇒ receive POLAR

Confirm the transaction. Your pETH is **permanently burned**, you receive newly minted POLAR, and any ETH rebate goes to your wallet.

### Stake, future-lock, or hold

Stake POLAR where the current app exposes POLAR Staking, wait for [vePOLAR](/stewardship/vepolar) locking once audited deployment publishes it, or hold it.

</Steps>

## What just happened

Your single transaction did four things at once:

- Burned pETH ⇒ raised the pETH [floor price](/peth/floor-price) under the curve assumptions.
- Released ETH from the bonding curve reserve.
- Paid you any ETH rebate and routed the rest to the [Fee Router](/stewardship/fee-router) as yield.
- Minted you new POLAR.

> **Warning:**
> Conversions are **one-way**. POLAR cannot be converted back to pETH, and burned pETH is gone for good. Confirm the previewed quote before signing.
