# Polaris Flows

Ecosystem incentivization is where most tokenomics go to die. The usual pattern: a DAO emits its native token, farmers capture it, dump it on long-term holders, and TVL evaporates when emissions stop. Backroom deals decide who gets subsidized.

**Flows are Polaris's replacement.** They distribute protocol revenue to contracts building on Polaris, denominated in hard assets such as pETH and pAssets, funded by perpetual revenue, and allocated by an auditable formula. No gauges, no bribes, no backroom deals, no emissions.

> **Current status:** Flow contracts, whitelists, observed-address rules, recipient rules, weights, fixed-minimum kickstarts, event signatures, and final parameter bounds are pending audited deployment. This page describes the intended model and operator requirements.

## The base rule

A whitelisted contract receives a Flow equal to its share of the underlying asset, relative to all other whitelisted contracts.

Hold 20% of the whitelisted pETH supply -> receive 20% of the pETH Flow, before any launch-specific adjustments. Holdings vote with their wallets; the formula does the rest.

## Two Flow families

| Flow | Recipients | Funded by | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **pETH Flow** | Eligible pETH-holding contracts such as Polaris CDPs or external integrations | Bonding-curve and conversion-related protocol revenue, subject to final routing | Pending |
| **pAsset Flow** | Eligible pAsset-holding contracts such as liquidity pools, vaults, markets, or structured products | Borrower interest or other pAsset revenue after the Stability Pool allocation, which sits above a hardcoded floor | Pending |

Each Flow has its own whitelist and parameters. The final contract reference will define which assets, recipients, and revenue sources go live at launch.

## Immutable by default, stewarded at the edges

The Flow implementation is intended to be immutable. In normal operation, stewards do not vote every epoch or steer a gauge; the formula computes distributions from eligible holdings.

vePOLAR's role is confined to the edges:

- **Whitelist.** A contract must be admitted by a stewardship vote before it can receive Flow revenue.
- **Weight.** A whitelisting can carry a bounded weight multiplier on top of raw ownership share.
- **Recipient.** A whitelist entry can distinguish the observed address from the address that receives revenue, if the final contracts support that separation.
- **Removal.** A participant that games the system can be removed per the published process.

> **Parameter status:** Whitelist fields, weight bounds, kickstart mechanics, Stability Pool floors, and removal rules are pending final publication. They are stewarded only within immutable bounds.

## Operator prerequisites

Teams seeking Flow participation should prepare:

- Product description and target users.
- The asset held: pETH or a specific pAsset.
- Observed contract address and recipient address, once deployed.
- Verified source links and ABI for the operator's own contracts.
- Upgrade/admin model for those contracts.
- How user deposits create durable Polaris asset demand.
- Requested Flow weight or kickstart, if applicable, with the published bounds shown.
- Monitoring plan, including how holdings and revenue are reported.
- Risk disclosure for users of the operator's product.

Final application templates and acceptance criteria are pending. Operators should not assume Flow eligibility until approved through the published stewardship process.

## Flows vs token emissions

The structural contrast is the reason Flows exist:

| Aspect | Emission gauges | Polaris Flows |
|---|---|---|
| **Currency** | Governance token, emitted and dilutive | Hard assets: pETH and pAssets |
| **Funding** | Constant token emissions | Perpetual protocol revenue |
| **Allocation** | Gauge votes, bribes, and recurring weight wars | Share of asset held, auto-computed |
| **Recipient** | Whoever farms the gauge fastest | Contracts whose users hold productive Polaris assets |
| **Stewardship load** | Epoch votes and lobbying | One onboarding vote plus bounded adjustments |
| **Sustainability** | Dries up when emissions end | Scales with protocol activity |

The only way new POLAR enters existence after inception is a [pETH burn](/polar/conversion-auctions). Flows keep ecosystem growth aligned with that constraint.


## Where Flows sit in the flywheel

Flows close the loop: deposits bring pETH, pETH backs CDPs, CDPs pay interest, interest feeds revenue-sharing paths, and those paths make integrators want to capture more pETH and pAssets. Growth in any part of the system can feed yield to other parts without a single POLAR emitted.

![The Polaris flywheel](/infographics/polaris-flywheel.svg)

By default, Flows should just work. Stewards step in only where human judgment is load-bearing: onboard a participant, set bounded terms, or remove one that no longer qualifies.
