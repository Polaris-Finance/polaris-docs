# Polaris Core Concepts

A single primitive, the bonding curve, produces pETH. pETH collateralizes every pAsset. Activity on any pAsset generates fees; fees burn pETH and raise its floor; a rising floor strengthens every position. POLAR captures the flywheel's long-term value and steers it.

This page is the map. Each term is defined in a sentence or two and links to its full treatment. If you read nothing else, read this.

## The assets

<DefinitionCard term="pAsset" token="passet">
A synthetic debt asset minted from a Polaris CDP (pUSD, pGOLD, pCHF, …). Each is overcollateralized by pETH and counterparty-free. See [The pAsset Catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="pUSD" token="pusd">
The flagship pAsset, pegged to the US dollar, backed by pETH collateral. See [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="pETH" token="peth">
ETH deposited into a two-way [bonding curve](/peth/bonding-curve). It is not CDP-minted debt; it is the <em>only</em> collateral Polaris accepts, has a floor price that is designed to rise under the curve invariants, and is yield-bearing.
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="POLAR" token="polar">
The protocol's stewardship token. After a fixed distribution at launch, new POLAR can be minted <em>only</em> by burning pETH in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions). <strong>vePOLAR</strong>, a vote-escrow layer for stewardship rights, is forthcoming.
</DefinitionCard>

## Minting & positions

<DefinitionCard term="CDP" token="default">
Collateralized Debt Position: the mechanism behind minting. You post collateral and mint the pAsset directly from the protocol, not by borrowing another user's funds. See [Minting pAssets](/minting/minting-passets).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Trove" token="default">
Your individual borrowing position in a Polaris CDP. Each trove is isolated, with its own collateral, debt, and collateralization ratio. See [Managing Your Trove](/minting/managing-your-trove).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="StablecoinOS" token="default">
Polaris is a <em>factory</em> of CDP instances, each issuing a different pAsset but all sharing the same pETH collateral and bonding curve.
</DefinitionCard>

## Keeping the peg

<DefinitionCard term="Redemption" token="default">
Exchanging a pAsset for its underlying collateral at face value (minus a fee) when it trades <em>below</em> peg. Restores the floor. See [Redemptions](/redemptions-liquidations/redemptions).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Minting (mechanism)" token="default">
The inverse of redemption. Arbitrageurs add pETH to troves to mint pAssets when the pAsset trades <em>above</em> peg. Restores the ceiling.
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Autonomous interest rates" token="default">
Redemption and minting volume serve as onchain proxy signals for the peg, letting Polaris set rates without an external oracle.
</DefinitionCard>

## Risk states

<DefinitionCard term="Liquidation" token="default">
If a trove falls below its minimum collateralization ratio, it is liquidated, and the [Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool) absorbs the debt. See [Liquidations](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Recovery Mode" token="default">
A protocol-wide protective state entered when the system-wide reserve-backing-to-debt ratio (the [TCR](/resources/glossary)) falls below a fixed threshold. See [Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Floor price / floor ratio" token="peth">
The mathematically enforced minimum price of pETH (in ETH), and that floor as a percentage of market price (your maximum drawdown). See [The Floor Price](/peth/floor-price).
</DefinitionCard>

## Yield & coordination

<DefinitionCard term="Stability Pool" token="default">
A reserve of pAssets that processes liquidations and is the primary venue for earning yield. See [The Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Conversion auction" token="polar">
The mechanism that mints POLAR by burning pETH; the most protocol-synergetic action possible. See [Conversion Auctions](/polar/conversion-auctions).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Fee Router" token="default">
Receives pETH from conversions and bonding-curve swap fees, then buffers and distributes it as yield to troves, pro-rata to their debt. See [The Fee Router](/stewardship/fee-router).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Stewardship" token="default">
A stewardship role steers <em>quantitative parameters within the bounds the contracts define</em>. It does not govern the immutable core. See [Stewardship, not Governance](/stewardship/stewardship).
</DefinitionCard>

## The shape of the system

![Polaris system overview: ETH swaps into pETH on the bonding curve, pETH collateralizes CDPs that mint pUSD, and burning pETH for POLAR raises the floor and releases ETH](/polaris-system-v2.svg)

*Key takeaway: one collateral primitive feeds the whole system; pETH creates collateral, CDPs mint pAssets, and POLAR conversion raises the pETH floor.*

The diagram traces four steps and an outcome:

1. **Swap.** ETH enters the **bonding curve** and is issued as **pETH**; the curve captures volatility.
2. **Collateral.** pETH is deposited as the sole collateral for **CDPs**.
3. **Borrow.** A CDP mints **pUSD** (or another pAsset) against that pETH, for scalable onchain yield.
4. **Stewardship.** Burning pETH in a conversion auction mints **POLAR**, raising the pETH floor price and releasing ETH.

> Adoption ⇒ activity ⇒ velocity ⇒ fees ⇒ yield ⇒ more demand.
