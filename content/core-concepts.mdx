# Polaris Core Concepts

## The shape of the system

<SystemOverviewFigure caption={null} />

*Key takeaway: one collateral primitive feeds the whole system; pETH creates collateral, branches mint pAssets, and POLAR conversion raises the pETH floor.*

The diagram traces four steps and an outcome:

1. **Swap.** ETH enters the **bonding curve** and is issued as **pETH**; the curve captures volatility.
2. **Collateral.** pETH is deposited as the sole collateral for pAsset **branches**.
3. **Borrow.** A user opens a **CDP** in a branch to mint **pUSD** (or another pAsset) against that pETH, for scalable onchain yield.
4. **Stewardship.** Burning pETH in a conversion auction mints **POLAR**, raising the pETH floor price and releasing ETH.

This page is the system map. Each term is defined briefly and links to its full treatment.

## The assets

<DefinitionCard term="pAsset" token="passet">
A synthetic debt asset minted from a Polaris branch through user CDPs (pUSD, pGOLD, pCHF, …). Each is overcollateralized by pETH and counterparty-free. See [The pAsset Catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="pUSD" token="pusd">
The flagship pAsset, pegged to the US dollar, backed by pETH collateral. See [Minting pAssets](/minting).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="pETH" token="peth">
ETH deposited into a two-way [bonding curve](/peth). It is not debt minted from a CDP; it is the <em>only</em> collateral Polaris accepts, has a contract-defined floor, and is yield-bearing.
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="POLAR" token="polar">
The protocol's stewardship token. After a fixed distribution at launch, new POLAR can be minted <em>only</em> by burning pETH in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions). <strong>vePOLAR</strong>, a vote-escrow layer for stewardship rights, is forthcoming.
</DefinitionCard>

## Minting & positions

<DefinitionCard term="CDB / Branch" token="default">
Collateralized Debt Branch: the aggregate protocol branch for one pAsset. A branch contains that pAsset's minting and redemption logic, debt accounting, risk parameters, Stability Pool, and all user CDPs. See [The pAsset Catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="CDP" token="default">
Collateralized Debt Position: your individual borrowing position inside a branch. You post pETH collateral and mint the pAsset directly from the protocol, not by borrowing another user's funds. Each CDP has its own collateral, debt, and loan-to-value (LTV). See [Minting pAssets](/minting).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Trove" token="default">
The Polaris app term for an individual CDP — the same position, by another name. See [Managing Your Trove](/minting/managing-your-trove).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="StablecoinOS" token="default">
Polaris's factory layer: each branch issues a different pAsset, all sharing the same pETH collateral and bonding curve. See [StablecoinOS: an open catalog](/minting/passet-catalog#stablecoinos-an-open-catalog).
</DefinitionCard>

## Keeping the peg

<DefinitionCard term="Redemption" token="default">
Exchanging a pAsset for its underlying collateral at face value (minus a fee) when it trades <em>below</em> peg. Restores the floor. See [Redemptions](/redemptions-liquidations).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Minting (mechanism)" token="default">
The inverse of redemption. Arbitrageurs add pETH to troves to mint pAssets when the pAsset trades <em>above</em> peg. Restores the ceiling.
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Autonomous interest rates" token="default">
Redemption and minting volume serve as onchain proxy signals for the peg, letting Polaris set rates without an external oracle.
</DefinitionCard>

## Risk states

<DefinitionCard term="Liquidation" token="default">
If a trove's LTV exceeds the maximum, it is liquidated, and the [Stability Pool](/yield) absorbs the debt — or, if the pool is too small, the debt and collateral redistribute to other troves. See [Liquidations](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Recovery Mode" token="default">
A protocol-wide protective state entered when the system-wide reserve-to-debt ratio (the [TCR, Total Collateralization Ratio](/resources#t)) falls below a fixed threshold. See [Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Floor price / floor ratio" token="peth">
The mathematically enforced minimum price of pETH (in ETH), and that floor as a percentage of market price (your maximum drawdown). See [The Floor Price](/peth/floor-price).
</DefinitionCard>

## Yield & coordination

<DefinitionCard term="Stability Pool" token="default">
A reserve of pAssets that processes liquidations and is the primary venue for earning yield. See [The Stability Pool](/yield).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Conversion auction" token="polar">
The mechanism that mints POLAR by burning pETH. See [Conversion Auctions](/polar/conversion-auctions).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Fee Router" token="default">
Buffers protocol revenue in pETH and streams it to troves pro-rata to debt (Flow recipients forthcoming); POLAR stakers are paid separately from borrower interest. Swap fees are first burned at the floor — raising it — and the released ETH is recycled into pETH. See [The Fee Router](/stewardship/fee-router).
</DefinitionCard>

<DefinitionCard term="Stewardship" token="default">
A stewardship role steers <em>quantitative parameters</em>, with safety-critical bounds hardcoded in the contracts and the remaining bounds set at audited deployment. It does not govern the immutable core. See [Stewardship, not Governance](/stewardship).
</DefinitionCard>
