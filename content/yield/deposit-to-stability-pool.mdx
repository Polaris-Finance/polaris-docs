# Deposit to the Polaris Stability Pool

This page walks through depositing a pAsset (pUSD in the examples) into the [Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool), claiming rewards, and withdrawing. If you have not yet, read [The Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool) first — it explains what you earn and the risk you take on.

<Callout variant="info" title="Where this appears in the app">
App anchor: **Earn** tab for direct deposits, or **Zap** tab when the app bundles the purchase and deposit.
</Callout>

> **Actionability:**
> Stability Pool deposits are testnet-actionable only on Sepolia using the current Public Testnet 1 manifests. Production deposits are not mainnet-actionable until [Polaris Launch Status](/launch-status) publishes the official app, production pool addresses, final reward/withdrawal parameters, and audit status. Screenshots and the exact UI flow are forthcoming; the steps below describe the intended flow and will get images at launch.

## Before you start

You need:

- A pAsset to deposit — most commonly **pUSD**. [Mint pUSD](/minting/minting-passets) first if you hold none.
- ETH for gas.
- An understanding that liquidation gains arrive in **pETH**, and the [risk](/yield/stability-pool#the-honest-risk) of a severe crash. For pure pUSD exposure, use a wrapper vault — see [Where the Yield Comes From](/yield/yield-sources).

## Before you sign

Before depositing, check:

- Official app URL, network, pAsset, and Stability Pool contract.
- Deposit amount, projected pool share, pool size, and reward tokens.
- Current APR estimate, liquidation parameters, and withdrawal conditions.
- First-loss exposure: liquidations can burn deposited pAssets and replace them with pETH.
- Whether you are comfortable receiving pETH during volatile liquidations.
- Gas, slippage, MEV, and wrapper-vault availability if you plan to convert pETH rewards back to pAsset.
- Approval spender, allowance amount, simulation result, and quote freshness.

> **Warning:**
> A Stability Pool deposit can lose value. Liquidations convert part of your deposited pAsset into pETH, and in a severe or flash crash the pETH you receive can be worth **less** than the pAsset burned — your principal is not protected. Size your deposit accordingly and read the [Risk Disclosure](/resources/risk-disclosure).

## Deposit

<Steps>

### Open the pool

Open the Polaris app and go to the **Stability Pool** for your pAsset (e.g. the pUSD pool).

### Enter a deposit amount

Enter the amount of pUSD to deposit.

### Approve the spend

**Approve** the pUSD spend, if prompted (a one-time token approval).

### Confirm the deposit

Confirm the **deposit** transaction in your wallet.

### Position is active

Your share of the pool is now active. It accrues borrower-interest yield immediately and is eligible for liquidation gains from the next liquidation onward.

</Steps>

## Claim rewards

Your two reward streams behave differently:

- **Interest share (pUSD)** accrues continuously to your position.
- **Liquidation gains (pETH)** accumulate to your position with each liquidation processed.

To realize them:

<Steps>

### Open your position

In the Stability Pool view, open your position to see **claimable pUSD** and **claimable pETH**.

### Claim or compound

Select **Claim** to withdraw rewards to your wallet, or compound them back into the pool where supported.

### Confirm reward action

Confirm the transaction in your wallet.

</Steps>

## Withdraw

<Steps>

### Choose Withdraw

In the Stability Pool view, choose **Withdraw**.

### Enter a withdrawal amount

Enter the amount of pUSD to remove, or select the full balance.

### Confirm withdrawal

Confirm the transaction in your wallet.

</Steps>

Withdrawing settles any unclaimed rewards. Your deposited pUSD balance fluctuates as liquidations convert part of it into pETH — what you withdraw reflects the pool's current state, not a fixed principal.
