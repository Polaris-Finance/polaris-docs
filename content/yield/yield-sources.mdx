# Polaris Yield Sources

Polaris yield comes from onchain protocol activity: borrowing, liquidations, swaps, and conversions. This page lists the sources; [Fee Router & Flows](/stewardship/fee-router) covers routing.

> Adoption ⇒ activity ⇒ velocity ⇒ fees ⇒ yield ⇒ more demand.

## The sources

| Source | Where it originates | Flows to |
|---|---|---|
| **Borrower interest** | Interest [trove](/minting/managing-your-trove) owners pay on minted pAssets | Primarily [Stability Pool](/yield) depositors, with stewarded splits where configured |
| **Liquidation gains** | The penalty on liquidated troves, paid in discounted pETH | Stability Pool depositors |
| **Bonding curve swap fees** | Fees on every ETH ⇄ pETH swap, buffered by the Fee Router and later burned at the [floor price](/peth/floor-price) | The pETH floor (via the burn); the released ETH is recycled into pETH and distributed to troves |
| **Conversion gains** | ETH released when pETH is burned to mint POLAR in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions) | pETH troves, pro-rata to recorded debt |

The first two drive [Stability Pool](/yield) yield and are the primary venue for pAsset holders. The bonding curve and conversion components make **pETH used as trove collateral yield-bearing** — the yield accrues to troves and to pETH's rising floor, not to a passive token balance — which is why using pETH as collateral can lower a borrower's net effective cost.

## Why this is "self-scaling"

These sources depend on protocol **activity**:

- More troves opened ⇒ more borrower interest.
- More volatility ⇒ more liquidation gains.
- More trading ⇒ more bonding curve swap fees.
- More POLAR minted ⇒ more conversion gains.

Yield scales with pAsset supply and protocol use, but realized returns still depend on activity, rates, liquidations, contract execution, and market conditions.

## Owned, not rented

Polaris yield is not subsidized, bridged in, or voted into being. It emerges from protocol use and is captured by the protocol itself.

## How yield is distributed

Conversion proceeds and swap fees go through the **[Fee Router](/stewardship/fee-router)**. Borrower interest follows separate pAsset-denominated split logic. Current values and pending production status live on [Parameters](/resources/testnet#parameters).

## Parameters

Use [Parameters](/resources/testnet#parameters) for current yield-related values. This page owns the source model, not numeric tables.
