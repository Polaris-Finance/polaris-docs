# Polaris Yield Sources

Most stablecoin yield is **rented** from offchain sources — T-bills, CEX lending, custodial strategies — which means counterparty risk and a hard ceiling: your yield is capped at whatever that counterparty delivers. Polaris yield is **owned**. Every source is onchain, internal to the protocol, and grows with usage.

> Adoption ⇒ activity ⇒ velocity ⇒ fees ⇒ yield ⇒ more demand.

## The sources

| Source | Where it originates | Flows to |
|---|---|---|
| **Borrower interest** | Interest [trove](/minting/managing-your-trove) owners pay on minted pAssets | Primarily [Stability Pool](/yield) depositors; a stewarded split also funds POLAR stakers (the Stability Pool and stakers each floored at 25%) and routes the remainder to the ecosystem fund, with secondary interest rebated to troves pro-rata to collateral |
| **Liquidation gains** | The penalty on liquidated troves, paid in discounted pETH | Stability Pool depositors |
| **Bonding curve swap fees** | Fees on every ETH ⇄ pETH swap, buffered by the Fee Router and later floor-sold and burned to raise the [floor price](/peth/floor-price) | pETH holders / troves |
| **Conversion gains** | ETH released when pETH is burned to mint POLAR in a [conversion auction](/polar/conversion-auctions) | pETH holders / troves |

The first two drive [Stability Pool](/yield) yield and are the primary venue for pAsset holders. The bonding curve and conversion components make **pETH used as trove collateral yield-bearing** — the yield accrues to troves and to pETH's rising floor, not to a passive token balance — which is why using pETH as collateral can lower a borrower's net effective cost.

## Why this is "self-scaling"

None of these sources depends on an external rate environment. They depend on **activity**:

- More troves opened ⇒ more borrower interest.
- More volatility ⇒ more liquidation gains.
- More trading ⇒ more bonding curve swap fees.
- More POLAR minted ⇒ more conversion gains.

Yield is therefore tied to protocol adoption, not to a counterparty's balance sheet. The mechanism scales with pAsset supply, but realized yield still depends on actual activity, rates, liquidations, contract execution, and market conditions. That is what "self-scaling" means: **activity ⇒ yield**, with no offchain dependency to compress or disappear.

## Owned, not rented

Polaris yield is not subsidized, bridged in, or discretionarily voted into being. It emerges from the protocol's own usage and is captured by the protocol itself — *internalized, not rented*. Because the core is intended to be immutable, no admin key should be able to swap these onchain sources for a T-bill desk under pressure after audited deployment; that architecture is designed to avoid the [degradation pattern](/why-polaris).

## How yield is distributed

Onchain fees do not flow straight to recipients: conversion proceeds and swap fees go to the **[Fee Router](/stewardship/fee-router)**, which buffers them and distributes pETH yield to troves pro-rata to recorded debt. POLAR Staking rewards and future [vePOLAR](/stewardship/vepolar) locker rewards are separate from that pETH stream. The stewarded primary-interest split that funds the Stability Pool, POLAR stakers, and the ecosystem fund is a distinct, pAsset-denominated stream. Exact values are finalized closer to launch.
