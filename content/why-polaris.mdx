# Why Polaris Finance

## The trilemma

Stablecoins are said to be **scalable, yield-bearing, or trustless — pick two**. Market structure mostly reinforces that framing: the largest stablecoins rely on issuers, custodians, offchain assets, or other counterparties, while cleaner trustless designs stay smaller.

That trade-off is real, but it is not a law of nature. It exists because, for years, high offchain interest rates made it *easy* to back a stablecoin with T-bills and CEX yield and bring that yield onchain. Easy, but not trustless — and not innovative.

## The degradation pattern

The deeper problem is **structural drift toward centralization**. Stablecoins tend to start relatively clean and get dirtier in how they source yield as they grow, because centralized backing is the path of least resistance to scale.

| Feature | **USDC** | **USDS / DAI** | **USDe** | **LUSD / BOLD** | **pUSD** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Issuer dependency | Centralized issuer with freeze/blacklist | Governance-set rates, RWA exposure | Market-neutral, custody-dependent | Counterparty-free | Counterparty-free |
| Yield source | Offchain reserves (not passed to holders) | Mixed: crypto + RWA + governance | Counterparty-dependent infrastructure | Limited onchain sources | Onchain protocol activity |
| Scalability constraint | Banking relationships | Governance decisions | Counterparty capacity | Capital inefficiency | Bonding-curve scaling |
| Immutable core | No | Partial | No | Yes | Yes |

<Callout variant="info">
The lesson: a stablecoin built for trustlessness must be <em>architected</em> so degradation cannot happen, not merely intend to stay clean.
</Callout>

## The counterparty-free lineage

Polaris descends from the line of immutable, counterparty-free CDP stablecoins, built to fix the limitation each prior generation hit.

![Polaris lineage: SAI (2017) ⇒ LUSD (2021) ⇒ BOLD / Liquity V2 (2024) ⇒ pUSD (2026)](/infographics/polaris-lineage.svg)

*The counterparty-free CDP lineage, 2017 to present.*

In text, the lineage reads:

1. **2017 — SAI:** the first CDP stablecoin, single-collateral. It later upgraded to DAI and then drifted toward centralized backing.
2. **2021 — LUSD:** immutable and decentralized, but capital-inefficient, and spent roughly 20% of its life more than 1% above peg.
3. **2024 — BOLD (Liquity V2):** a refined CDP mechanism from the original Liquity developers — still trustless, still yield-constrained.
4. **2026 — pUSD:** the next chapter. A bonding-curve collateral design that solves the overpeg problem and unlocks onchain-scaling yield without adding a counterparty.

## How Polaris resolves it

Polaris is built so the only counterparty you trust is **immutable code and Ethereum**:

- **Onchain-only yield.** Every yield source — borrower interest, bonding curve swap fees, conversion gains, PolarEX swap fees — is internal to the protocol and grows with activity. Nothing is rented from a CEX or T-bill desk.
- **Immutable core.** The bonding curve, CDP logic, liquidations, redemptions, and the Stability Pool are intended to be fixed in audited deployment code. No admin key can add a custodial PSM, external-collateral module, or freeze function under pressure.
- **Counterparty-free by construction.** pUSD sits in the same counterparty-free lineage as LUSD and BOLD — but adds yield that *scales* with DeFi volume rather than capping growth at what a counterparty can deliver.

<Callout variant="warning">
Having a counterparty means capping your growth to whatever yield that counterparty can deliver.
</Callout>

Polaris turns onchain trading volume itself into a stablecoin yield source through its bonding curve, and extends the model to *any* asset through the [StablecoinOS](/core-concepts). DeFi has had uncensorable-but-small and censorable-but-scalable stablecoins. Polaris is built to be the uncensorable, scalable one.
