# Why Polaris

## Manifesto

Decentralized finance was built on the idea that money should not depend on trusted intermediaries. A permissionless financial layer open to everybody, where participation depends on transparent rules rather than centralized institutions.

Over the years, stablecoins have become the foundation of this onchain economy, enabling everything from trading and lending to payments and settlement.

However, their biggest tradeoff is that most rely on centralized issuers that control the underlying reserves, retain the yield those reserves generate, and ultimately keep ownership over the monetary infrastructure, including the ability to freeze assets.

At the same time, others evolved beyond simple digital dollars into yield-bearing financial products whose returns depend on external counterparties and increasingly complex strategies, becoming more of a tokenized derivative than a true currency.

Both approaches helped DeFi grow and demonstrated strong product-market fit. Yet they gradually reintroduced the very dependencies the ecosystem was built to remove.

The result is a fundamental contradiction.

Blockchains gave us financial infrastructure that anyone can access and verify, yet the currencies flowing through it often depend on centralized institutions or on risks users cannot fully evaluate.

We still have not answered one of the fundamental questions in crypto: what should permissionless money look like at global scale?

## Why pETH

The evolution of stablecoins revealed another structural problem.

Even if permissionless money could scale without relying on centralized issuers or opaque sources of yield, Ethereum would still face a different challenge. The network secures one of the largest financial ecosystems in the world, yet very little of the activity on top of it meaningfully strengthens ETH itself.

Today, staking is the primary source of native ETH yield, as it plays a fundamental role in securing the network. But as DeFi expands, staking should not be the only way for ETH to participate.

Polaris is built on a simple observation: Ethereum’s monetary layer and its yield layer should not be separate systems.

For now, the two operate independently. Stablecoins generate enormous economic activity, while the collateral supporting them rarely benefits from it. Meanwhile, ETH secures the network that makes this economy possible, yet captures little of the value created above it.

Polaris connects these two systems by placing its collateral, pETH, at the center of its yield layer.

As demand for the stablecoins and synthetic assets permissionlessly issued on Polaris grows, the resulting protocol activity is designed to reinforce pETH, the reserve asset supporting them. Instead of importing yield from external dependencies, Polaris redirects value created within its own economy back into the system through liquidity flows.

pETH derives its yield from the economic activity generated within Polaris itself. As the reserve asset of the protocol, it allows the collateral backing the monetary layer to participate directly in the value created by that economy.

This distinguishes it from liquid staking tokens, whose yield comes from securing the Ethereum network.

## Vision

Stablecoins are the largest monetary application built on blockchains today, and USDp is Polaris’ path toward a stable, censorship-resistant dollar that can finally scale. But once a scalable reserve asset exists, its role goes far beyond issuing a single currency.

As more assets are built on pETH, the same reserve foundation can support currencies, commodities and synthetic assets that all share one infrastructure.

As the ecosystem expands, the reserve asset can evolve alongside it. Splitting pETH into fpETH and vpETH is one example, letting users unlock entirely new strategies. Over time, new primitives can keep extending that foundation.

The same principle applies to the applications built above it. Polaris is designed as infrastructure rather than a closed ecosystem, allowing third-party builders to permissionlessly issue their own stablecoins, synthetic assets and financial applications.

In turn, every application built on Polaris contributes to the same pETH-based reserve foundation, so builders benefit from an ecosystem that grows stronger as it expands.

This also changes how real-world value reaches the blockchain. Rather than importing traditional financial infrastructure, with its custodians and trust assumptions, Polaris provides a foundation where those same currencies, commodities and assets can exist while preserving the properties that made blockchains valuable in the first place: transparency, censorship resistance, immutability and permissionless access.

If this model succeeds, ETH itself can play a broader role within Ethereum. Staking will always remain fundamental to securing the network, but it should not be ETH’s only productive destination.

By introducing a native yield layer, pETH creates another productive path for ETH, giving holders and stakers a new choice while letting the monetary system built on Ethereum expand.

In that future, Ethereum’s growth and its monetary infrastructure no longer evolve independently.

The more the ecosystem grows, the stronger its reserve foundation becomes. And the stronger that foundation, the more it can support the next generation of DeFi applications.

## Design Choices

| Choice | Why it exists | What it enables |
| --- | --- | --- |
| pETH as common collateral | Gives every pAsset market the same ETH-backed reserve foundation | USDp, GOLDp, and future pAssets can scale from the same collateral reserve, making risk easier to understand across the system |
| Two-way bonding curve | Gives pETH native liquidity without depending on external liquidity providers | ETH can enter and exit through transparent onchain rules while fees and burns can raise the pETH floor. |
| Earn Vaults | Turn liquidation processing and issuer activity into yield opportunities | pAsset holders can earn yield while providing an additional layer of resilience to the system. |
| POLAR stewardship | Allows decision-making within predefined boundaries while keeping the core logic immutable. | Long-term coordination without rewriting the base protocol |
| pETH split | Separates floor exposure from upside exposure | Users can choose different risk and return profiles |
| Direct pAsset issuance | Lets users create pAssets directly from the protocol against pETH collateral | New pAssets can scale from native demand without relying on external lenders. |
| Redemptions | Help defend pAsset pegs below their reference value | Below-peg pressure can be corrected without offchain redemption promises. |
| Peg-responsive interest rates | Adjust issuer costs based on each pAsset's peg conditions | Helps balance supply and demand while routing value back to Earn Vaults and POLAR stewardship. |
