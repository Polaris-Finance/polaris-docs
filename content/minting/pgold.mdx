# GOLDp

GOLDp is a **pAsset that tracks the price of one ounce of gold**, issued against pETH collateral rather than bullion in a vault. It is decentralized gold exposure with no KYC, no size minimums, and no counterparty other than the Polaris protocol itself - no vault in Singapore, just a price feed and pETH collateral.

> **Note:** GOLDp is Testnet-active on Sepolia. See [Launch Status](/launch-status) for the current list of live pAssets.

## GOLDp vs centralized gold (XAUT/PAXG)

GOLDp and the incumbent gold tokens both give gold-price exposure, but they are structurally different products.

| Property | GOLDp | XAUT / PAXG |
|---|---|---|
| Collateral | pETH, fully onchain | Physical gold in custody |
| Redemption | Permissionless, no protocol-level minimum published yet | Issuer-gated; account requirements and redemption minimums depend on the issuer's current terms |
| KYC | None | Required to redeem |
| Physical custody | None - nothing to store or insure | Vaulted bullion, with custody risk |
| Counterparty | Polaris protocol only | Tether / Paxos as issuer |

![GOLDp versus centralized gold tokens (XAUT/PAXG): GOLDp is backed by onchain pETH with permissionless, KYC-free access and Polaris as the only counterparty, while XAUT/PAXG are backed by vaulted bullion with issuer-gated, KYC-required redemption and custody risk](/infographics/pgold-vs-centralized-gold.svg)

Under current issuer terms, the two centralized tokens together hold roughly \$5B in market cap, and redemption is gated behind KYC plus fees and minimums - XAUT, for example, charges 0.25% and requires roughly one gold bar (430 XAUT, about \$2.1M) to redeem. GOLDp inherits ETH-collateral and smart-contract risk where XAUT/PAXG inherit custodial and issuer risk. What GOLDp removes is the permissioned issuer layer.

GOLDp's collateral pricing composes two oracle feeds with the bonding curve: pETH is valued in gold terms as ETH/USD × the curve's pETH price ÷ gold/USD. A failure of either feed affects GOLDp pricing; see [Oracle risk](/resources/risk-disclosure#oracle-risk).

## Borrowing GOLDp is not shorting gold

![Borrowing GOLDp is not shorting gold: deposit pETH and borrow GOLDp, then your strategy sets the exposure - hold or LP the gold pair for gold-neutral exposure, or sell GOLDp for pETH (or loop) to take short-gold exposure](/infographics/pgold-borrowing-not-shorting.svg)

Issuing GOLDp against pETH is a borrowing action; whether you end up long pETH, gold-neutral, or short gold depends on what you do with the GOLDp. Borrow 1 GOLDp and hold it and your gold exposure is neutral - you owe 1 GOLDp and hold 1 GOLDp. The position only becomes a short if you sell that GOLDp for a non-gold asset.

So almost any view on gold can be expressed with GOLDp:

| Your view | How to express it |
|---|---|
| **Long gold** | Buy GOLDp and LP a gold-correlated pair |
| **Short gold, long ETH** | Post pETH, borrow GOLDp, then sell it or loop the position |
| **Long gold, short ETH** | Buy GOLDp and deposit it in the [Earn Vault](/yield) |
| **Gold-neutral, long volume** | Borrow GOLDp and LP gold-correlated pairs |

## Why borrow GOLDp

Polaris is designed to make borrowing attractive: borrowers pay interest but also receive pETH incentives on their position, so the *effective* rate can run low or even negative when incentives exceed interest. Because gold sees strong holding demand but comparatively little borrow demand, GOLDp is a natural fit for that flexibility. See [Yield Sources](/yield/yield-sources) and [POLAR Tokenomics](/polar/tokenomics) for the mechanics.
