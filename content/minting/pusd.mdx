# USDp

USDp is Polaris's flagship dollar pAsset.

It is designed to track the US dollar while being backed by pETH collateral instead of bank deposits, T-bills, custodians, or offchain issuers.

USDp belongs to the same trust-minimized stablecoin family as LUSD and BOLD: its peg is supported by onchain incentives, not an offchain issuer.

Note: USDp is Testnet-active on Sepolia. See [Launch Status](/launch-status) for the current list of live pAssets.

## Why Use USDp

USDp is designed for users who want dollar-denominated onchain exposure without relying on a centralized stablecoin issuer or taking issuer counterparty risk.

Users can:
- Create USDp against pETH collateral.
- Hold USDp as a decentralized dollar asset.
- Deposit USDp into an Earn Vault to earn scalable, protocol-native yield.
- Use USDp across supported Polaris and DeFi integrations.

## How USDp Is Created

USDp is created inside Polaris against pETH collateral.

Users can create USDp from the Issue tab by opening a position. USDp can also be created through above-peg arbitrage when market conditions make issuance attractive.

Both paths expand USDp supply against pETH collateral.

See [Issuing pAssets](/minting), [Peg Defense](/redemptions-liquidations), and [Manage A Position](/minting/manage-position).

## Peg Support

USDp peg support comes from issuance above peg and redemptions below peg.

Those flows expand or contract supply through arbitrage, and Polaris also uses them as the onchain signal for autonomous interest rates.

See [Peg Defense](/redemptions-liquidations) and [Interest Rates](/minting/interest-rates).

## Earn With USDp

USDp holders can deposit into the USDp Earn Vault to earn protocol-native yield from issuer interest and liquidation gains.

The Earn Vault is also the first-loss backstop for USDp liquidations, so deposits carry liquidation-processing risk.

See [Earn Vaults](/yield).

## Main Risks

USDp reduces issuer and offchain counterparty risk compared with centralized stablecoins, but it still carries protocol risk.

Main risks include smart-contract risk, pETH collateral price risk, liquidation risk for issuers, issuance and redemption exposure for open positions, Earn Vault risk for depositors, oracle risk, MEV, frontend risk, and Ethereum execution risk.

Read [Risk Disclosure](/resources/risk-disclosure).
