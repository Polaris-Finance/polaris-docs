# Open a Polaris Trove

import { Steps } from 'nextra/components'


A **trove** is your individual CDP: its own collateral, debt, and loan-to-value (LTV). Opening one is how you mint a pAsset: **connect wallet ⇒ acquire pETH ⇒ deposit pETH as collateral ⇒ choose and mint a pAsset ⇒ confirm ⇒ monitor your LTV**. This page walks each step; for the concepts behind it, see [Minting pAssets](/minting) and [Managing Your Trove](/minting/managing-your-trove).

<Callout variant="info" title="Where this appears in the app">
App anchor: [**Borrow** tab](/using-app/borrow) -> pAsset trove flow. If the app says CDP, it is referring to the same individual pAsset debt position this page calls a trove.
</Callout>

For the full **Borrow / CDB / CDP / trove / reserve loan** vocabulary — including why a reserve loan is not a pAsset trove — see [Borrower terminology](/minting#borrower-terminology).

## Before you start

- A wallet with the right asset for the environment: **Sepolia ETH** for Public Testnet 1 or **ETH on Ethereum mainnet** after production launch.
- A target pAsset in mind — see [the catalog](/minting/passet-catalog).


## Parameters

Current testnet parameters and production-finality status live on [Parameters](/resources/testnet#parameters). Use the app quote and live contract state before signing.

## Steps

<Steps>

### Connect your wallet

Open the Polaris app and connect.

### Acquire pETH

Deposit ETH into the [bonding curve](/peth) to receive pETH. pETH is the *only* collateral Polaris accepts. If you already hold pETH, skip this step.

### Deposit pETH as collateral

Choose how much pETH to lock in the trove. This collateral stays in the trove until you withdraw it. Opening also takes a small fixed pETH deposit on top of your collateral as liquidation gas compensation — returned in full when you close the trove, paid to the liquidator only if the trove is liquidated. Its current size lives on [Parameters](/resources/testnet#parameters).

### Choose pAsset and amount

Pick the pAsset (pUSD, pGOLD, …) and the amount of debt to take on. The app shows the resulting **loan-to-value (LTV)** — your debt value divided by your collateral value — before you confirm.

### Confirm

Sign the transaction. The minted pAsset arrives in your wallet; the debt is recorded against your trove.

### Monitor your LTV

Your LTV moves with the price of ETH and with any changes you make. Watch it from the trove dashboard and adjust before it nears the maximum. See [Managing Your Trove](/minting/managing-your-trove).

</Steps>

## Choose an LTV buffer

Your LTV is the number that determines liquidation risk. Keep enough room below the maximum to absorb ETH price moves, interest accrual, gas delays, and redemption impact.

Redemptions are different from liquidations: lower LTV does not exempt your trove from proportional redemptions, but it can keep the smaller post-redemption position away from the liquidation line. See [Managing Your Trove](/minting/managing-your-trove) for buffer strategy and examples.
