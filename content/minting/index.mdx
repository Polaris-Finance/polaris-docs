# Minting Polaris pAssets

## Branches and CDPs

Polaris issues each pAsset through a **Collateralized Debt Branch (CDB)**. A branch is the aggregate protocol layer for one pAsset; each borrower opens an individual **Collateralized Debt Position (CDP)** inside that branch. From the outside it looks like borrowing against collateral, but underneath it differs from a money market like Aave.

- A **money market** matches lenders and borrowers through a shared pool. You borrow funds another user supplied; an interest rate floats with pool utilization.
- A **CDP** lets each borrower **mint** the pAsset directly from the protocol. You aren't borrowing anyone's deposit; you're your own mini central bank: post collateral ⇒ mint a pAsset ⇒ owe that debt back.

Each CDP is isolated in a **[trove](/minting/managing-your-trove)** with its own collateral, debt, and loan-to-value (LTV). Users of a given pAsset share only (1) the branch's pAsset and peg state, and (2) branch-wide parameters like the [Total Collateralization Ratio and Recovery Mode](/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode). That isolation bounds the blast radius: each branch keeps its own debt accounting, so trouble in one cannot reach the troves or collateral of another.

## Borrower terminology

| Term you may see | Docs term | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Borrow** | User action | The app tab/action for taking on debt. |
| **CDB / Branch** | Aggregate | The pAsset-level protocol branch: token, minting/redemption logic, accounting, parameters, Stability Pool, and all user CDPs. |
| **CDP** | Position | Your individual pAsset borrowing position: collateral, debt, and LTV. |
| **Trove** | App/docs term | The Polaris name for an individual CDP. |
| **Loan / Reserve Loan** | Separate product | A WETH loan against fpETH floor-value collateral, not a pAsset trove. |

When these docs say **trove**, they mean a pAsset debt position. When the app says **Reserve Loan**, read [pETH Split: fpETH and vpETH](/peth/split) instead; reserve loans use fpETH, not pETH trove collateral.

### Strengths and trade-offs

The CDP model is ~8 years proven, its strengths and weaknesses well understood; Polaris applies it through pAsset branches, amplifying the former and dampening the latter.

| Strengths | Trade-offs |
|---|---|
| **Trustless** — logic fully enforced onchain, no intermediaries | **Liquidation risk** — collateral below threshold can be [liquidated](/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations) |
| **Transparent** — ratios and liquidation logic auditable onchain | **Volatility** — sharp collateral drops can trigger liquidations |
| **Optionality** — unlock liquidity without selling your assets | **Smart-contract risk** — mitigated by audits, never zero |
| **Debt-asset agnostic** — the minted asset can be anything | **Capital inefficiency** — overcollateralization leaves capital idle |

## What Polaris does differently

### Collateral is pETH, and only pETH

Polaris does not accept wstETH or other staked-ETH tokens: they would be external dependencies that cap scaling. It accepts only **[pETH](/peth)**, ETH deposited into the bonding curve. This dampens the CDP model's capital-inefficiency weakness, because pETH is:

1. an ETH proxy with a contract-defined floor, and
2. **yield-bearing** — collateral pETH also earns from other parts of the system (e.g. ETH released by conversions).

### Autonomous, market-aware interest rates

Setting branch interest rates is where most designs struggle: Maker still uses governance-set rates (spiky, unpredictable); Liquity v1 had none (sustained supply pressure as T-bill rates rose); Liquity v2 pushes rate-setting onto users.

Polaris sets rates **autonomously, without a direct market-price feed for the pAsset**, by reading signals already inside the system:

- **Redemptions are profitable when pUSD < \$1** ⇒ a spike in redemption volume signals a downward depeg ⇒ raise the rate.
- **Minting is profitable when pUSD > \$1** ⇒ a spike in minting volume signals an upward depeg ⇒ lower the rate.

This gives the protocol an internal peg-pressure signal, not a substitute for every oracle in the system. Collateral valuation, non-dollar pAsset reference units, stale-price checks, and liquidation math still depend on the final audited pricing paths for those assets. See [Redemptions](/redemptions-liquidations) for the mechanism behind the peg-pressure signal.
