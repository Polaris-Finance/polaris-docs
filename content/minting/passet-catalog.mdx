# Polaris Asset Catalog

A **pAsset** is a synthetic debt asset minted from a Polaris CDP against pETH collateral. Each pAsset is overcollateralized, counterparty-free, and redeemable through onchain protocol mechanics under the usual assumptions: functioning contracts, live Ethereum execution, available gas, and valid pricing data. **pETH is different:** it is not minted from a trove; it is the bonding-curve collateral token that backs pAssets. The catalog is open: through [StablecoinOS](#stablecoinos-an-open-catalog), credible teams can add new pAssets, all sharing the same pETH collateral and bonding curve.

> **Current Public Testnet 1 focus:** pUSD and pGOLD are Testnet-active on Sepolia. pCHF, pBigMac, and other catalog ideas are illustrative or future until [Launch Status](/launch-status) says otherwise.

## Polaris assets

| Asset | Tracks | Current status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **pUSD** | US dollar | **Testnet-active** | The flagship pAsset. Counterparty-free dollar with yield that scales with protocol activity. See [Minting pAssets](/minting). |
| **pETH** | ETH | **Testnet-active collateral** | *Special.* Not minted from a trove: it is the collateral itself, produced by the [bonding curve](/peth) with a floor price designed to rise under the curve assumptions. It backs every other pAsset. |
| **pGOLD** | 1 ounce of gold | **Testnet-active** | Decentralized gold exposure, backed by pETH rather than bullion. Public Testnet 1 uses Sepolia testnet price-feed plumbing; see the comparison below. |
| **pCHF** | Swiss franc | **Illustrative / future** | A non-USD fiat peg, demonstrating that the model is debt-asset agnostic across currencies. Not Testnet-active unless Launch Status later lists it. |
| **pBigMac** | The [Big Mac Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Mac_Index) | **Illustrative / future** | A novelty pAsset tracking a Big Mac's price, proof that Polaris can mint anything with a price feed and a flexible enough fee flow. Not Testnet-active unless Launch Status later lists it. |

## pGOLD vs centralized gold (XAUT/PAXG)

pGOLD and the incumbent gold tokens both give gold-price exposure, but they are structurally different products.

| Property | pGOLD | XAUT / PAXG |
|---|---|---|
| Collateral | pETH, fully onchain | Physical gold in custody |
| Redemption | Permissionless, no protocol-level minimum published yet | Issuer-gated; account requirements and redemption minimums depend on the issuer's current terms |
| KYC | None | Required to redeem |
| Physical custody | None — nothing to store or insure | Vaulted bullion, with custody risk |
| Counterparty | Polaris protocol only | Tether / Paxos as issuer |

The trade-off is honest: pGOLD inherits ETH-collateral and smart-contract risk where XAUT/PAXG inherit custodial and issuer risk. What pGOLD removes is the *permissioned* layer: no counterparty can freeze, blacklist, or gate your redemption.

## StablecoinOS: an open catalog

The CDP model is **debt-asset agnostic**: it can mint a synthetic pegged to any asset with robust reference pricing and risk parameters. Most protocols ship one stablecoin and stop. Polaris is built as a *factory* of CDP instances under **StablecoinOS**: one CDP per pAsset, all sharing the same pETH collateral pool and bonding curve.

That shared collateral does double duty. It lets credible teams add a new pAsset (pEUR, pSilver, or something stranger) without bootstrapping a fresh collateral base. And because each instance has its own debt asset, a violent day in one market (a gold spike, say) drives liquidations on that instance while the others barely move. The pETH collateral is mutualized; the risk is not.

The result is a catalog that grows without diluting the safety of what already exists.
