## What you need

- **An Ethereum wallet** — any standard EOA or smart-account wallet.
- **For Public Testnet 1:** Sepolia ETH for gas, plus test WETH/reserve tokens from the app faucet when the faucet is enabled for your wallet. Testnet assets have no monetary value.
- **For production later:** ETH on Ethereum mainnet — the only asset you need to begin; Polaris converts it to [pETH](/peth/bonding-curve) for you.

That's the whole list. No KYC, no whitelist, no offchain account.

## Sepolia ETH, test WETH, and pETH

On Public Testnet 1, three "ETH-like" assets can appear in the flow:

- **Sepolia ETH** pays gas on Sepolia. Get it from a public Sepolia faucet; it is not mainnet ETH.
- **Test WETH / reserve token** is the mock reserve token in the Sepolia manifest. The app faucet can mint it when enabled for your wallet. It is not mainnet WETH and has no value.
- **pETH** is the Polaris bonding-curve collateral token. On Public Testnet 1, it is Sepolia-only pETH created from the test reserve token; it is not production pETH.

The first testnet path is: open the [Public Testnet 1 app](https://app.testnet.polarisfinance.io/), switch to Sepolia, mint test WETH/reserve tokens if the faucet is available, swap them through the bonding curve for pETH, borrow or mint a Testnet-active pAsset, then optionally deposit that pAsset into the Stability Pool to test Earn.

## The basic flow

In one line: **deposit ETH ⇒ receive pETH ⇒ open a trove and mint a pAsset ⇒ optionally deposit to the Stability Pool to earn yield.** On Public Testnet 1 this uses Sepolia test assets; in production it uses Ethereum mainnet once launch status says mainnet is live. Step by step:

1. **Deposit or swap into pETH.** On Public Testnet 1, use the app faucet for mock WETH/reserve tokens when available, then use the bonding-curve / swap surface to receive pETH. In production later, this starts from mainnet ETH.
2. **Receive pETH from the [bonding curve](/peth/bonding-curve).** Your input asset is swapped for pETH at the curve's current price: mock WETH/reserve tokens on Public Testnet 1, and ETH in production later. pETH is an ETH proxy with a floor price that only rises, and the only collateral Polaris accepts.
3. **Open a [trove](/minting/open-a-trove) and mint a pAsset.** Post your pETH as collateral, then choose which pAsset to mint. pUSD and pGOLD are Testnet-active; pCHF and pBigMac are illustrative or future catalog examples. The pAsset lands in your wallet; the debt stays in your trove. See [the catalog](/minting/passet-catalog) for what you can mint.
4. **Optionally, earn yield.** Deposit your pAsset into the [Stability Pool](/yield/stability-pool) to earn a share of borrower interest plus discounted pETH from liquidations. This is optional — you can instead hold, LP, or use your pAsset anywhere in DeFi.

## Launch verification

Before you connect a wallet or sign a transaction, verify:

- For Public Testnet 1, the app URL is exactly [https://app.testnet.polarisfinance.io/](https://app.testnet.polarisfinance.io/).
- For production/mainnet, wait until [Polaris Launch Status](/launch-status) publishes the production app URL.
- The app is connected to the intended network: Sepolia (`11155111`) for Public Testnet 1, or Ethereum mainnet (`1`) only after production launch.
- Testnet contract addresses match [Public Testnet 1](/resources/testnet) and the linked Sepolia manifests.
- Production contract addresses, verified source, ABIs, and audit status match [Contracts and Addresses](/resources/contracts) and [Audits and Security](/resources/audits-security) once production launches.
- The product page you're following has been updated from conceptual flow to launch parameters.
