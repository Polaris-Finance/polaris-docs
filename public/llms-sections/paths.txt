# Borrow Polaris pAssets

**Who this is for:** anyone who wants to mint pUSD, pGOLD, or another pAsset against pETH collateral and actively manage that position.

**Prerequisites:** an Ethereum wallet, test assets on Sepolia for Public Testnet 1 or ETH on Ethereum mainnet after production launch, and a working grasp of overcollateralization and liquidation. If those terms are new, read [Core Concepts](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/core-concepts) first.

> **Launch status:** Borrowing is testnet-actionable on Sepolia only when using the official Public Testnet 1 manifests. Production borrowing is not mainnet-actionable until [Polaris Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status) publishes the app URL, production addresses, final collateral thresholds, and audit status. Do not connect to a frontend or contract that claims to be Polaris unless it is linked from these docs or another official Polaris channel.

> **Terminology:** In the app, **Borrow** is the task label. Docs call a pAsset debt position a **trove**; contracts may call the mechanism a **CDP**. **Reserve loans** are separate WETH loans against [fpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/split), not pAsset troves.

## Reading sequence

Read these in order — each step builds context the next one assumes.

1. [Getting Started](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/getting-started) — confirms your wallet, network, and launch status so you avoid an unverified deployment.

3. [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/minting-passets) — explains the CDP model and the peg mechanics that determine your interest rate.
4. [Open a Trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/open-a-trove) — the conceptual flow for posting collateral and minting your pAsset.
5. [Managing Your Trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/managing-your-trove) — how collateralization, debt, and interest move over time, and how to stay clear of liquidation.
6. [Liquidations](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations) and [Recovery Mode](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode) — the failure states that decide how conservatively to size your position.

- The pETH, pAsset, trove manager, and Stability Pool addresses.

## Convert, Stake, and Future-Lock POLAR

URL: https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/paths/convert-lock-polar

# Convert, Stake, and Future-Lock POLAR

**Who this is for:** long-term participants who want to acquire POLAR through conversion, use current POLAR Staking where available, and understand forthcoming vePOLAR locking.

**Prerequisites:** pETH to burn in a conversion auction, and acceptance that conversion is one-way — you cannot un-burn pETH. To understand what your burn contributes, read [The Floor Price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price) first.

> **Launch status:** Conversion and current staking are testnet-actionable only where Sepolia artifacts exist. Production conversion, staking, and vePOLAR locking are not mainnet-actionable until [Polaris Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status) publishes the official app, POLAR, staking, and vePOLAR addresses, lock durations, voting process, final parameter bounds, and audit status. Do not use an unofficial conversion, staking, or locking interface.

## Reading sequence

Read these in order — understand the token and the irreversible burn before committing pETH.

1. [The POLAR Token](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/polar-token) — POLAR's role and why, after the initial distribution, new POLAR can be minted *only* by burning pETH.
2. [Conversion Auctions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions) — the mechanism and its risks; read before burning any pETH to mint POLAR.
3. [Participate in a Conversion](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/participate-in-conversion) — the conceptual user flow for a conversion.
4. [vePOLAR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/vepolar) — how future locking will differ from current POLAR Staking.
5. [Stewardship, not Governance](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/stewardship) — the hardcoded bounds your vote operates within; read before voting on any proposal.

## POLAR Staking vs vePOLAR

POLAR Staking is the current app staking/rewards surface where exposed. vePOLAR is forthcoming vote-escrow locking. Once live, vePOLAR can steward bounded parameters, vote on permitted onboarding decisions, and receive a stewarded share of protocol yield. It cannot upgrade contracts, change the bonding curve, change liquidation or redemption logic, create emissions gauges, or mint POLAR except through pETH conversion.

## What to verify at launch

- The official POLAR, conversion auction, and vePOLAR addresses.
- The official POLAR Staking address and claim path, if staking is exposed.
- Verified source and ABI for each contract.
- Final lock duration choices and vote-power decay rules.
- Final proposal, voting, quorum, timelock, and execution rules.
- The hardcoded parameter bounds for any proposal you vote on.

## Build now, bind later

Until deployment artifacts ship, the safest useful work is interface planning:

- Model the product concepts: pETH, pAssets, troves, Stability Pools, Fee Router, POLAR, and vePOLAR.
- Design data schemas around stable conceptual entities, not unpublished function names.
- Build verification gates so your app refuses unverified networks or addresses.
- Keep chain-specific config external and replaceable.
- Track the official docs for the deployment manifest, ABIs, and event catalog.

## Gotchas

- **Testnet addresses are not production addresses.** Treat Sepolia manifests as replaceable fixtures. Never use them as defaults for mainnet.
- **One pAsset means one instance.** A pUSD trove and a pGOLD trove are unrelated positions with different managers, pools, parameters, and risk states.
- **pETH is collateral, not pAsset debt.** Do not put pETH in the same accounting bucket as CDP-minted pAssets.
- **Redemptions are global to an instance.** There is no sorted lowest-ICR queue. If you model borrower exposure, model pro-rata reduction across all open troves for that pAsset.
- **Fee Router pETH rewards and primary-interest splits are separate streams.** Do not assume every yield destination is paid by the same contract or denomination.
- **Final event names are pending.** Build your indexer so event bindings are generated from the published ABI, not copied from draft examples.

## Failure modes to handle

Failure mode | Integration response

Wrong chain ID | Refuse reads/writes and show the expected chain from [Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status).
Address missing from the manifest | Disable the feature rather than falling back to a search result or environment default.
Explorer source or ABI mismatch | Treat the deployment as unverified and fail closed.
Stale oracle or unavailable price path | Hide leverage/borrowing actions and show risk state as unknown.
Recovery Mode active | Block or warn on collateral-reducing and debt-increasing flows according to live contract state.
Redemption, liquidation, or conversion quote changes before inclusion | Requote before signing and use explicit user slippage bounds.
Unknown pAsset instance | Do not share pUSD assumptions with another pAsset; load per-instance parameters.

## Worked integration sketch

The exact package, ABI, and method names are pending. The important pattern is a fail-closed deployment gate before any read or write.

This is integration pseudo-code, not a published SDK. The shape is copy-safe TypeScript, but the explorer-verification implementation must come from your own stack or a future Polaris SDK after canonical ABIs publish.

```ts
type Address = `0x${string}`

type PolarisDeployment = {
  chainId: number
  contracts: Record<string, Address>
  abiHashes: Record<string, string>
}

type ReadClient = {
  getBytecode(args: { address: Address }): Promise<`0x${string}` | null | undefined>
}

type ExplorerVerifier = (args: {
  address: Address
  expectedAbiHash: string
}) => Promise<boolean>

const EXPECTED_CHAIN_ID = 11155111 // Public Testnet 1 today; production mainnet is 1 after launch.
```

```ts

  client: ReadClient,
  deployment: PolarisDeployment,
  verifyExplorerSourceAndAbiHash: ExplorerVerifier
) {
  if (deployment.chainId !== EXPECTED_CHAIN_ID) {
    throw new Error(`Unexpected Polaris network: ${deployment.chainId}`)
  }

  for (const [name, address] of Object.entries(deployment.contracts)) {
    const code = await client.getBytecode({ address })
    if (!code) throw new Error(`Missing bytecode for ${name}`)

    const verified = await verifyExplorerSourceAndAbiHash({
      address,
      expectedAbiHash: deployment.abiHashes[name]
    })
    if (!verified) throw new Error(`Unverified Polaris contract: ${name}`)
  }

  return {
    peth: deployment.contracts.peth,
    pUSDManager: deployment.contracts.pUSDManager,
    pUSDStabilityPool: deployment.contracts.pUSDStabilityPool
  }
}
```

Use the same shape for production later, but with Ethereum mainnet chain ID `1` and only after [Contracts & Addresses](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/contracts) publishes canonical production artifacts.

# Earn Yield on Polaris

**Who this is for:** holders who want protocol-native yield from pAssets, pETH, current POLAR Staking, or future locked POLAR — without token emissions, bribes, or offchain backing.

**Prerequisites:** a pAsset to deposit (so most readers come here after [Borrow pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/paths/borrow-passets)) or pETH, plus awareness that Stability Pool yield carries liquidation-conversion exposure to your principal.

> **Launch status:** Yield flows are conceptual and testnet-actionable only where Sepolia artifacts exist. Production deposits are not mainnet-actionable until [Polaris Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status) publishes the official app, production pool addresses, final splits, buffering parameters, and audit status.

## Reading sequence

Read these in order — start with where the yield originates before committing capital.

1. [Where the Yield Comes From](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield/yield-sources) — the full source map, so you can judge whether each yield stream is sustainable rather than subsidized.
2. [The Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield/stability-pool) — the primary venue for pAsset yield, and how liquidations can convert your deposit into discounted pETH.
3. [Deposit to the Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield/deposit-to-stability-pool) — the user flow you will follow once the app is public.
4. [The Fee Router](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/fee-router) — how pETH from fees and conversions reaches configured receivers, and how that differs from pAsset-denominated staking rewards.
5. [Flows](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/flows) — only if you are an integrator or operator seeking protocol revenue sharing; safe to skip as a plain depositor.

## What yield is not

Polaris yield is not a token-emission program, gauge market, bribe market, T-bill wrapper, or CEX lending strategy. It comes from protocol activity: borrower interest, liquidation gains, bonding-curve fees, conversion gains, and other onchain fees described in the yield docs.

## What to verify at launch

- The official app URL.
- The Stability Pool address for the pAsset you deposit.
- The final interest split and liquidation parameters for that pAsset.
- The Fee Router address and verified source.
- Whether any wrapper vault you use is official, audited, and listed by the project that operates it.

Production Ethereum mainnet (`1`) is forthcoming, and no production app, production addresses, ABIs, verified source, final parameters, or audit reports are published yet.

> **Open Testnet App:** Use [https://app.testnet.polarisfinance.io/](https://app.testnet.polarisfinance.io/) only on Sepolia (`11155111`) with no-value test assets. Treat any production frontend, token, contract, airdrop, or support message as unverified until it is linked from [Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status).
