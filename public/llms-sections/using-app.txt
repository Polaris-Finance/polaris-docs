# Dashboard & Analytics

The **Dashboard** is the app's orientation view. It is where you check balances, open positions, pending rewards, and system health before choosing a transaction tab. The **Analytics** view, covered further down, shows the protocol-wide metrics behind those numbers.

App anchors: **Dashboard** for your own state, and **Analytics** for protocol-wide context. Use both for inspection only; execute actions from the matching task tab after checking a fresh quote.

Image: Polaris Dashboard Earn cards: pUSD and pGOLD Stability Pools, POLAR Staking, and vpETH Staking, each showing APR, deposited or staked amount, and pending gains

*Each Earn card shows its APR, your deposited or staked amount, and the gains accrued so far across each reward token.*

## What to check

Area | What it tells you

**Balances** | WETH, pETH, fpETH, vpETH, pAssets, POLAR, and claimable rewards held by the connected wallet
**Borrow positions** | Open troves, reserve loans, debt, collateral, and health metrics
**Earn positions** | Stability Pool deposits, POLAR Staking balances, and claimable gains
**System status** | Recovery Mode, floor ratio, borrow rates, pool size, and other launch/runtime signals
**Next actions** | Which tabs can adjust or close the position you are viewing

## Read before acting

- A dashboard number is a state read, not a transaction quote.
- A position can change between the Dashboard and the moment your transaction lands.
- Use the transaction tab's quote and simulation before signing.

## Analytics: protocol-wide metrics

Use **Analytics** to inspect protocol-wide state before deciding on a transaction. Analytics is for context and monitoring; action tabs provide the final quote.

Metric | Why it matters

**Floor price** | The contract-defined pETH floor under the bonding-curve assumptions
**Floor ratio** | pETH floor price divided by current pETH market price; frames curve-implied drawdown
**Borrow APR** | Annualized estimate of current pAsset debt cost
**Stability Pool size** | Available first-loss buffer for liquidations
**Recovery Mode status** | Whether a pAsset instance is under system-wide stress restrictions
**PSM/redemption activity** | Peg pressure and fee context for pAsset markets
**POLAR Staking rewards** | Current routed rewards where the staking surface is live

Before relying on a metric:

- Check the timestamp or refresh state before acting.
- Distinguish an APR estimate from realized yield.
- Confirm whether a metric is testnet-only, illustrative, or production-published.
- Use the action tab's current quote for fees, slippage, and final amounts.

# Borrow

Use **Borrow** for two distinct position types:

App anchor: **Borrow** tab. Trove actions appear as pAsset borrowing/CDP actions; reserve loans appear as fpETH-backed WETH loan actions.

Image: Polaris Borrow tab: pUSD selected, 10 pETH collateral at 75% LTV, with a projected position showing liquidation price, estimated monthly cost, and LTV

*Pick the pAsset to mint, set collateral and LTV, then review the projected position — liquidation price, monthly cost, and LTV — before approving.*

App wording | Docs wording | What it means

**Borrow pAssets** | **Open or adjust a trove** | Deposit pETH collateral and mint pUSD, pGOLD, or another pAsset as debt.
**CDP** | **Trove / CDP instance** | The contract mechanism behind a pAsset borrow position.
**Loan / Reserve Loan** | **Reserve loan** | Borrow WETH against fpETH floor-value collateral; this is not a pAsset trove.

For the full **Borrow / CDP / trove / reserve loan** vocabulary, see [Borrower terminology](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting#borrower-terminology).

# Earn

Use **Earn** for protocol-native yield surfaces: Stability Pool deposits, reward claims, and the current **POLAR Staking** surface where available.

App anchor: **Earn** tab. Stability Pool deposits and POLAR Staking controls live here when the current deployment exposes them.

Image: Polaris Earn tab: pUSD Stability Pool deposit form at 15.2% APR, with a deposit amount entered and a checkbox to also claim pending pUSD and pETH gains

*Each surface has its own tab; the Stability Pool deposit form shows APR, your balance, and an option to claim pending gains alongside the deposit.*

## Earn surfaces

Surface | What you deposit | What you receive | Read first

**Stability Pool** | pUSD, pGOLD, or another pAsset | Borrower-interest share and liquidation gains | [Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield)
**POLAR Staking** | POLAR | Routed staking rewards where the current deployment exposes them | [POLAR Tokenomics](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/tokenomics)
**vePOLAR locking** | POLAR, once locking is deployed | Stewardship rights and a future yield path | [vePOLAR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/vepolar)

POLAR Staking and vePOLAR are not the same thing. The current staking surface is a stake/unstake/claim flow. vePOLAR locking is a forthcoming vote-escrow mechanism with lock durations, vote-power decay, and stewardship rights.

## Split

URL: https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/using-app/split

# Split

Use **Split** to divide pETH into two matching legs:

App anchor: **Split** tab. Use it to split pETH into fpETH + vpETH or merge equal fpETH/vpETH balances back into pETH.

Image: Polaris Split tab: 10 pETH splitting into 10 fpETH and 10 vpETH, with a toggle between Split and Merge modes

*Splitting 10 pETH yields 10 fpETH and 10 vpETH; the Merge toggle reverses it when you hold equal balances of both legs.*

```
pETH -> fpETH + vpETH
```

Each unit of pETH split mints one unit of **fpETH** and one unit of **vpETH**. Holding both legs together is economically similar to holding the original pETH, before fees and execution effects. Holding only one leg changes your risk.

## What each leg represents

Token | Role

**fpETH** | The floor-price leg. It can be redeemed through the splitter for WETH at the pETH floor-price value under the contract assumptions. It is also the collateral for reserve loans.
**vpETH** | The premium or volatility leg. It represents exposure to pETH's market value above the floor and can become worth very little if the premium compresses.

For the full mechanism and risks, read [pETH Split: fpETH and vpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/split).

## Swap

URL: https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/using-app/swap

# Swap

Use **Swap** when you want to exchange WETH, pETH, fpETH, vpETH, or a pAsset through the route the app quotes. For pETH, the route may involve the [bonding curve](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth); for pAssets, it may route through app-supported markets or protocol modules.

App anchor: **Swap** tab. Use it for WETH -> pETH, pETH -> WETH, pAsset routes, and any quoted route that shows price impact or slippage before signing.

Image: Polaris Swap tab: selling 5 ETH for pETH, showing the quoted output, minimum received, exchange rate, and a route of WETH through the bonding curve to pETH

*The quote shows output, minimum received, and rate; the route panel makes the path explicit — here WETH through the bonding curve to pETH.*

## Common swaps

Goal | Typical route | Read first

Get pETH | WETH -> pETH | [pETH Bonding Curve](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth)
Exit pETH | pETH -> WETH | [pETH Floor Price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price)
Get a pAsset to deposit | WETH -> pUSD or pGOLD | [Polaris Asset Catalog](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/passet-catalog)
Rebalance before a debt action | pAsset or pETH swap | [Managing a Trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/managing-your-trove)

## Risks

Swapping does not remove pETH price risk, pAsset peg risk, or execution risk. The final amount can differ from the preview because of gas, slippage, MEV, route changes, and transaction ordering.

## Zap

URL: https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/using-app/zap

# Zap

Use **Zap** when the app bundles several steps into one guided flow. The main yield zap is:

App anchor: **Zap** tab. Treat each bundled step as its own economic action even if the app presents one guided flow.

```
WETH -> pUSD or pGOLD -> Stability Pool deposit
```

The zap is an execution shortcut. It does not change the economics of the underlying swap or deposit.

## What the zap does

1. Takes a WETH amount as input.
2. Quotes the pAsset purchase, such as pUSD or pGOLD.
3. Quotes the Stability Pool deposit for that pAsset.
4. Simulates the bundled operation.
5. Prompts for approvals and transaction signatures.

Depending on the implementation, the app may ask for one transaction or several. Treat every approval and every signature as a separate action.

## Failure modes

A zap can fail because one leg changes before inclusion: slippage, depleted liquidity, stale oracle data, Recovery Mode restrictions, approval mismatch, or gas limits. If it fails after an approval but before the final deposit, inspect your wallet balances before trying again.
