## A

**Advanced**
The app area for expert, permissionless operations: PSM mint/redeem of pUSD and pGOLD, pETH-to-POLAR conversion, and the testnet faucet. Use it only after verifying the contract, function, token, amount, and quote; see [Advanced](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/using-app/advanced).

**APR (Annual Percentage Rate)**
An annualized rate estimate, commonly shown for borrow cost or yield. APR is not a promised return: realized cost or yield can change with rates, activity, liquidations, fees, execution, and market conditions.

## B

**Bonding curve**
The two-way curve that mints and burns [pETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth), giving it a contract-native buy/sell path with no liquidity providers. See [pETH Bonding Curve](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth) and [pETH Floor Price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price).

## C

**CDP (Collateralized Debt Position)**
The mechanism behind minting: post collateral, mint a pAsset directly from the protocol rather than borrowing another user's funds. Each position is an isolated [trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#t). See [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting).

**Conversion auction**
The mechanism that burns [pETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth) to mint new [POLAR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar). See [Conversion Auctions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions).

**Converter rebate**
A stewardship-controlled percentage of ETH returned to the converter in a [conversion auction](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions). The dial between conversion volume and system yield.

## F

**Fee Router**
Receives protocol revenue, buffers it, and routes it to configured yield paths. See [Fee Router & Flows](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/fee-router).

**Floor Carry**
The possible return from holding [fpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#f) while the pETH [floor price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#f) rises. It is not a fixed APR or a guarantee; it depends on actual floor growth, market pricing, execution costs, and the redemption path remaining available.

**Floor price**
The contract-defined minimum price of [pETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth), in ETH terms, under the bonding-curve assumptions. See [pETH Floor Price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price).

**Floor ratio**
The floor price divided by the market price, as a percentage. It frames the curve-implied drawdown before gas, slippage, MEV, smart-contract risk, and chain-liveness risk. See [The Floor Price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price).

**Flows**
The protocol's internal value movements (fees, conversions, yield distribution), routed by the [Fee Router](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#f). Inward-facing flows sustain the core; outward-facing flows grow the ecosystem (see *Inward-facing / Outward-facing*).

**fpETH**
The floor-price leg created when [pETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/split) is split into fpETH and [vpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#v). fpETH can be redeemed for WETH at the pETH floor-price value under the splitter and bonding-curve assumptions, and it is used as reserve-loan collateral.

## G

**Guide**
The app area that routes users by goal, such as getting pETH, borrowing pAssets, earning yield, splitting pETH, or verifying safety. See [Polaris App](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/using-app).

## I

**ICR (Individual Collateralization Ratio)**
The protocol-side inverse of [LTV](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#l): pETH collateral value divided by pAsset debt. The app shows LTV; if ICR falls below the minimum, the equivalent LTV has crossed the maximum and the trove can be [liquidated](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

**Inward-facing / Outward-facing**
The design split between core mechanics handled by pETH/pAssets and ecosystem growth handled by [POLAR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar).

## L

**LTV (loan-to-value)**
The app-facing health metric for a [trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#t): pAsset debt divided by pETH collateral value. Lower LTV is safer; if LTV exceeds the maximum, the trove can be [liquidated](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

**Liquidation**
The process that closes an undercollateralized [trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#t): when its [LTV](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#l) exceeds the maximum, the [Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield) absorbs its debt and receives its collateral — or, if the pool is too small, the debt and collateral redistribute to other troves. See [Liquidations](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

## M

**Minting (mechanism)**
The inverse of [redemption](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#r): when a pAsset trades *above* peg (e.g. pUSD > \$1), arbitrageurs add pETH to troves to mint the pAsset and sell it, pushing the price down. This restores the ceiling and is one of the onchain signals used to set interest rates. See [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting).

**Minting (verb)**
Creating a pAsset by opening a [trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#t) and drawing pAsset debt against pETH collateral. Polaris *mints* pAssets, not "issues" or "creates" them.

## O

**Oracle**
An onchain feed used to price ETH collateral. Peg awareness uses minting and redemption volume as internal signals; address status lives on [Contracts & Addresses](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet#contracts-and-addresses).

## P

**pAsset**
A Polaris synthetic debt asset minted from a CDP — pUSD, pGOLD, pCHF, and beyond. Each is overcollateralized by pETH and counterparty-free. pETH itself is the collateral token, not CDP-minted pAsset debt. See [The pAsset Catalog](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/passet-catalog).

**pBigMac**
A novelty pAsset demonstrating the "mint anything" thesis: if a price feed exists, Polaris can mint a pAsset that tracks it.

**pCHF**
The Polaris synthetic Swiss franc — a pAsset pegged to CHF.

**pETH**
ETH deposited into the two-way [bonding curve](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth), and the only collateral Polaris accepts for pAsset troves. See [pETH Bonding Curve](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth).

**pGOLD**
The Polaris synthetic gold pAsset, designed to track gold without issuer custody or KYC at the base protocol layer. See [The pAsset Catalog](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/passet-catalog).

**Pathfinders**
Early Polaris contributors and community members selected through public contribution paths.

**Polar Council**
A planned treasury-funded program for onboarding and ecosystem contributors. Terms are pending publication.

**POLAR**
The protocol's stewardship token and outward-facing growth lever. After the initial distribution, new POLAR can be minted *only* by burning pETH in a [conversion auction](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions). See [The POLAR Token](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar).

**POLAR Staking**
The current app surface for staking POLAR and claiming routed rewards where exposed. It is distinct from forthcoming [vePOLAR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#v) locking.

**PSM (Peg Stability Module)**
The contract/module that handles pAsset minting and redemption mechanics for a pAsset instance. Use PSM or redemption actions only after checking the pAsset, fee, output amount, and contract address.

**pUSD**
The flagship pAsset, designed to track the US dollar and be backed by pETH collateral. See [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting).

## R

**Recovery Mode**
A protective state triggered when a pAsset's system-wide reserve-to-debt ratio falls below a threshold. It restricts risk-increasing borrower actions; liquidation eligibility is unchanged. See [Recovery Mode](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).

**Redemption**
Exchanging a pAsset for its underlying collateral at face value (minus a fee) when the pAsset trades *below* peg. Redemptions restore the floor and are one of the onchain signals used to set interest rates. See [Redemptions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations).

**Reserve Loan**
A WETH loan against [fpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#f) floor-value collateral. It is separate from a pAsset trove: reserve loans use the pETH floor leg and reserve accounting, while troves mint pAssets against pETH collateral.

## S

**sepETH**
Sepolia ETH, the testnet-native gas asset used on Public Testnet 1. It has no mainnet ETH value; when wrapped for app flows it may appear as WETH.

**SP (Stability Pool)**
Common abbreviation for the [Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#s), the pAsset first-loss pool that absorbs liquidations and pays depositors borrower-interest share and liquidation gains.

**Split**
The app flow and contract operation that turns pETH into equal amounts of [fpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#f) and [vpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#v), or merges equal fpETH and vpETH amounts back into pETH. See [pETH Split](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/split).

**Spike-and-decay**
The pricing model for [conversion auctions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions): the conversion price continuously decays until a conversion *spikes* it upward, proportionally to the POLAR supply that conversion inflated.

**Stability Pool**
A reserve of pAssets that absorbs the debt of [liquidated](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations) troves and is the protocol's primary venue for earning yield. See [The Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield).

**StablecoinOS**
The CDP-factory layer: many distinct pAssets, each issued by its own CDP instance, all sharing one pETH collateral and bonding curve. See [Core Concepts](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/core-concepts).

**Stewardship**
The bounded role stewards play: tuning *quantitative parameters within the bounds the contracts define*. The immutable core cannot be changed; stewards steward, they do not govern. See [Stewardship, not Governance](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship).

## T

**TCR (Total Collateralization Ratio)**
The system-wide reserve-to-debt health metric for a pAsset. Dropping below the configured threshold triggers [Recovery Mode](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).

**Trove**
An individual borrowing position in a Polaris CDP. Each trove is isolated, with its own collateral, debt, and [LTV](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#l). Polaris uses *trove*, never "vault" or "position". See [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting).

## V

**vePOLAR**
The forthcoming vote-escrow layer for POLAR, used for [stewardship](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship) rights and a yield path. See [vePOLAR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/vepolar).

**vpETH**
The volatility-premium leg created when [pETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/split) is split into [fpETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources#f) and vpETH. vpETH represents exposure to pETH's market value above the floor and can lose significant value if the premium compresses.

## W

**WETH**
Wrapped ETH, the ERC-20 form of ETH used by app contracts and testnet flows. On Sepolia, WETH is a test token backed by testnet ETH, not mainnet ETH.

## Z

**Zap**
An app flow that bundles multiple steps, such as WETH -> pUSD or pGOLD -> Stability Pool deposit. A zap is an execution shortcut, not a risk reducer; check approvals, simulation, slippage, quote freshness, and first-loss risk. See [Zap](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/using-app/zap).

# Polaris Audits & Security

> **Pre-launch note:** Audits and verifications are ongoing; this page will be updated with major developments.

## The security model

Polaris's intended security model rests as much on what it *cannot* do as on what it can:

- **Immutability.** The core contracts are intended to be non-upgradeable after deployment. This removes upgrade-key and proxy-admin attack surface, raising the stakes on getting the code right: immutability cuts both ways — it cannot be tampered with, and it cannot be patched.
- **No admin keys.** The design has no freeze, blacklist, or pause functions for anyone, including the team. No privileged key is intended to move user funds.
- **Bounded stewardship.** Stewards adjust only quantitative parameters — many of them bounded by hardcoded ranges (for example interest-rate maxima, fee-split floors, and the mint/redemption decay factors) — and cannot reach the core logic. See [Stewardship, not Governance](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship).
- **Narrow oracle dependency.** Peg awareness uses internal redemption and minting volume rather than a pAsset price oracle. Collateral pricing still requires an ETH price feed; the provider, feed address, and network details are not published until launch.

For the full accounting of what can still go wrong, read the [Risk Disclosure](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/risk-disclosure).

## Usage

**Do**

- Treat **dark mode as the canonical surface**: design against the navy palette first.
- Use **Star (`#E8DCC4`)** for body text, not pure white.
- Keep **borders subtle**: low-contrast navy edges, never heavy outlines.
- Give the emblem clear space; let it breathe.

**Don't**

- Don't use **pure white (`#FFFFFF`)** for text — too harsh against the navy. Use Star or Star bright.
- Don't recolor the logos or the token colors.
- Don't stretch, rotate, or add effects to the marks.
- Don't place the logo on a busy or low-contrast background.

# Polaris Finance FAQ

FAQ answers are intentionally short. Follow the linked canonical page when you need exact status, addresses, parameters, or risk details.

## General

### What is Polaris?

Polaris is a pETH-powered stablecoin operating system. ETH becomes pETH through a bonding curve, pETH collateralizes pAssets such as pUSD and pGOLD, and protocol activity routes yield through onchain mechanisms. Start with [Core Concepts](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/core-concepts).

### What is live now?

The current phase is owned by [Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status). As of June 2026, Polaris is in Public Testnet 1 on Sepolia (`11155111`), and production/mainnet use is not live.

### Which app should I use?

Use only the app listed on [Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status). For Public Testnet 1, follow [Public Testnet Quickstart](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/quickstart).

## Using the app

### How do I get started on testnet?

Open the official testnet app, connect Sepolia, use test assets only, swap into pETH, then try Borrow, Earn, Split, Zap, or Advanced as needed. The step-by-step path is [Public Testnet Quickstart](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/quickstart).

### Why does the app show Borrow if docs say mint?

The app says Borrow because the user action creates debt. The mechanism is a CDP/trove: you deposit pETH collateral and mint a pAsset directly from the protocol. See [Borrow](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/using-app/borrow) and [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting).

### What should I do if a quote or transaction fails?

Use [Troubleshooting](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/troubleshooting). Most failures come from wrong network, stale quotes, insufficient allowance, insufficient gas, Recovery Mode restrictions, or changed state before inclusion.

## Contracts, parameters, and safety

### Where are contract addresses?

[Contracts & Addresses](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet#contracts-and-addresses) is the address owner. Public Testnet 1 addresses are Sepolia artifacts; production addresses are not published until Launch Status says production is live.

### Where are current parameters?

[Parameters](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet#parameters) owns numeric values. Task pages link there instead of repeating tables.

### Where are testnet manifests?

[Public Testnet 1 Artifacts](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet) owns manifest paths and testnet artifact sources.

### What should I verify before signing?

At minimum, verify frontend URL, network, chain ID, address, token, spender, amount, and action.

## Risk

### Can I lose funds?

Yes. Polaris involves smart-contract risk, collateral price risk, liquidation risk, Stability Pool first-loss risk, MEV risk, oracle risk, and frontend/phishing risk. Read [Risk Disclosure](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/risk-disclosure).

### What is LTV?

LTV is loan-to-value: your trove's debt divided by its collateral value. Lower LTV is safer. Protocol parameters may also show the inverse ratio, ICR or MCR; for example, 150% ICR equals 66.7% LTV. See the [Glossary](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources) and [Managing Your Trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/managing-your-trove).

### What is TCR or Emergency Mode?

TCR is the system-level reserve-to-debt health metric for a pAsset. If it drops below the configured threshold, Recovery Mode tightens risk-increasing borrower actions. See [Recovery Mode](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode) and [Parameters](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet#parameters).

## Security

### Is Polaris audited?

Audit status is owned by [Audits & Security](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/audits-security). Until final reports are linked there, do not rely on audit claims found elsewhere.

### Is there a bug bounty?

Bug-bounty and disclosure status are owned by [Audits & Security](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/audits-security). Do not send exploit details to private forms, DMs, or third-party pages unless the official security page links them.

# Polaris Risk Disclosure

Polaris is trustless, immutable infrastructure. That design choice has real costs, and we disclose them before we claim the benefits. Every risk below is paired with the mechanism that mitigates it (*mitigates*, not eliminates). Read this page in full before you mint, deposit, or lock anything.

> **Note:** Nothing here is financial advice. Polaris is currently in the phase shown on [Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status). Testnet assets have no monetary value, and production use is not live unless Launch Status says it is. Trustlessness means the protocol will not, and cannot, step in to make you whole.

## Smart-contract risk

Polaris lives entirely onchain. Its bonding curve, CDPs, Stability Pool, and conversion auctions are smart contracts, and any smart contract can contain bugs.

**Mitigation.** The intended core is immutable and admin-key-free, removing upgrade and governance-key attack surface but raising the stakes on getting the code right. Final audit reports are not published in these docs yet; until reports appear on [Audits & Security](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/audits-security), treat the protocol as unaudited. Immutability cuts both ways: it cannot be tampered with, and it cannot be patched. Residual risk is never zero.

## Collateral and price risk

The only collateral is [pETH](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth), which is **volatile against ETH**. pETH trades at a varying premium above its [floor price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price), and that premium can compress sharply in a drawdown.

**Mitigation.** The [floor ratio](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price) frames curve-implied downside in ETH terms before execution and technical risk. Your risk is set at entry: the spread between your entry price and the floor, plus contract and chain assumptions.

> **Warning:** A high floor ratio does not protect against ETH's own volatility. pETH's floor is denominated in ETH terms. If ETH itself falls against the dollar, your pETH falls with it.

## Oracle risk

Pricing pETH collateral for liquidation requires an ETH price feed. A stale, manipulated, or failed oracle could misprice collateral and trigger wrong liquidations, or fail to trigger correct ones.

**Mitigation.** Peg awareness uses redemption and minting volume as internal signals for each pAsset (see [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting)). The oracle dependency is confined to pricing ETH collateral. Current testnet and production address status lives on [Contracts & Addresses](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet#contracts-and-addresses).

## Peg and depeg risk

A pAsset can trade away from its peg, below it in a panic or above it in a demand spike. Depegs can be temporary or, under severe stress, sustained.

**Mitigation.** Two opposing arbitrage mechanisms defend the peg structurally:

- **[Redemptions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations)** make it profitable to buy the pAsset below peg and redeem it for collateral, pushing the price up toward \$1.
- **Minting** makes it profitable to mint and sell the pAsset above peg, pushing the price back down.

Because both pay arbitrageurs the moment the peg slips, they activate without anyone's permission. pUSD is a counterparty-free stablecoin in the same trust-minimized family as LUSD and BOLD, so the peg is not propped up by a redeemable offchain reserve; it is held by onchain incentives.

## Liquidation risk (borrowers)

If your [trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting)'s loan-to-value (LTV) rises above the maximum, the trove is [liquidated](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations): you lose your collateral to cover the debt. Protocol parameters may publish the inverse threshold as a minimum collateral ratio (MCR).

**Mitigation.** Liquidation logic is transparent and auditable onchain, with no discretionary calls. You control your LTV: post more collateral, or repay debt, to stay clear of the threshold. pETH's rising floor and yield-bearing nature dampen, but do not remove, the volatility that causes liquidations. Monitor your trove actively, especially in volatile markets.

## Stability Pool first-loss risk (depositors)

Depositing pAssets into the [Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield) is the primary way to earn yield, but the Pool is the system's **first-loss buffer**. When a trove is liquidated, your deposited pAssets are burned to cancel its debt, and you receive the liquidated pETH collateral in exchange.

**Mitigation.** Liquidations are designed to occur while the trove is still overcollateralized, so depositors typically receive collateral worth *more* than the pAssets they gave up, and that surplus is the source of the Pool's yield. The risk is the opposite case: a liquidation processed below the value of the burned deposit, or a sharp collapse in the received collateral's value. You take on liquidation-processing risk in exchange for yield; size your deposit accordingly.

## Recovery Mode

When the system-wide reserve backing relative to total debt (the [reserve-to-debt ratio, shown as the TCR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources)) drops below a threshold, the protocol enters [Recovery Mode](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode), a defensive state with tighter requirements.

**Mitigation.** Recovery Mode is a feature, not a failure: it raises the bar for the whole system to rebuild collateralization quickly. Borrowers bear the cost: in Recovery Mode you cannot withdraw collateral or take on more debt unless you stay below the stricter recovery max LTV and do not increase your LTV (pure collateral top-ups and repayments stay allowed). Liquidation eligibility itself does not change; it stays at the normal max LTV in every mode. Keep your LTV comfortably below the maximum so a stress event does not box in your ability to act.

## Stewardship and economic risk

Stewards control quantitative parameters: the interest splits, conversion-price parameters, and the [converter rebate](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources). Parameter changes can affect your yield and your position's economics.

**Mitigation.** Stewardship is constrained: many economic parameters move only within hardcoded ranges, and the core logic cannot be changed by vote. See [Stewardship, not Governance](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship) and [Parameters](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet#parameters) for the current scope.

## MEV risk

Redemptions, liquidations, and [conversion auctions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions) are profitable, permissionless actions, so they attract MEV searchers and arbitrage bots. Ordinary users competing by hand will usually lose the race to automation.

**Mitigation.** MEV competition helps execute stabilizing actions, but ordinary users should not expect to win arbitrage races manually. Use the protocol for the position you want, not to front-run bots.

## Dependency risk (Ethereum)

Polaris is currently in Public Testnet 1 on Sepolia, and production is intended for Ethereum mainnet (chain ID `1`) once launched. Production Polaris inherits Ethereum's risks: consensus failures, severe congestion, or a contentious fork.

**Mitigation.** This dependency is deliberate and unhedged: the only counterparties Polaris is meant to ask you to trust are immutable code and Ethereum itself. We do not spread across chains to dilute that exposure, because doing so would reintroduce the bridge and external-dependency risks Polaris exists to avoid.

## Frontend and phishing risk

Fake frontends, fake contract addresses, and fake support channels can appear before users know what the official ones look like. A malicious frontend can ask you to approve tokens, sign dangerous messages, or connect to the wrong network.

**Mitigation.** Use only the frontend, network, and addresses listed in these docs. Use no production app URL, production network, production contract address, audit claim, or bug-bounty link unless it is listed here. Never enter a seed phrase, private key, or recovery words into any Polaris-related site. The current status checklist lives on [Polaris Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status) and [Public Testnet 1](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/testnet).
