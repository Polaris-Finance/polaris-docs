# Polaris Finance Documentation

Polaris is the **pETH-powered yield layer for all of DeFi**, a self-scaling stablecoin operating system. It produces yield-bearing **pETH** and uses it as the base collateral for minting synthetic assets (**pAssets**) through onchain debt positions.

The infrastructure is trustless and immutable, and every pAsset is backed entirely by onchain collateral and yield, with no T-bill or CEX dependency. The result is a competitive, risk-adjusted yield source for both ETH and stablecoins. Through **Flows**, third-party protocols can build on Polaris assets and share in protocol yield.

This documentation explains how the system works, how to use it, and the trade-offs it makes. For the current network, contract, and actionability status, start with [Launch Status](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/launch-status).

## The three engines

Polaris is three products that each stand on their own, but whose real power is their synergy. The bonding curve makes pETH work. The CDP makes pAssets work. The conversion mechanism and POLAR make them work together:

Engine | What it is | Why it matters

**1. pETH** | ETH deposited into a two-way **bonding curve**, with a floor price designed to rise under the curve invariants | A capital-efficient, yield-bearing collateral with a contract-defined exit path under stated assumptions
**2. pAssets** | Synthetic pegged (USD, Gold, etc.) assets minted against collateral, via a Collateralized Debt Position (CDP) | Counterparty-free stables that scale with onchain activity
**3. POLAR** | The stewardship token, minted only by burning pETH in a **conversion auction** | Aligns long-term incentives and steers the protocol within hardcoded bounds

# Polaris Core Concepts

This page is the map. Each term is defined in a sentence or two and links to its full treatment. If you read nothing else, read this.

## The assets

A synthetic debt asset minted from a Polaris CDP (pUSD, pGOLD, pCHF, …). Each is overcollateralized by pETH and counterparty-free. See [The pAsset Catalog](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/passet-catalog).

The flagship pAsset, pegged to the US dollar, backed by pETH collateral. See [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/minting-passets).

ETH deposited into a two-way [bonding curve](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/bonding-curve). It is not CDP-minted debt; it is the <em>only</em> collateral Polaris accepts, has a floor price that is designed to rise under the curve invariants, and is yield-bearing.

The protocol's stewardship token. After a fixed distribution at launch, new POLAR can be minted <em>only</em> by burning pETH in a [conversion auction](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions). <strong>vePOLAR</strong>, a vote-escrow layer for stewardship rights, is forthcoming.

## Minting & positions

Collateralized Debt Position: the mechanism behind minting. You post collateral and mint the pAsset directly from the protocol, not by borrowing another user's funds. See [Minting pAssets](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/minting-passets).

Your individual borrowing position in a Polaris CDP. Each trove is isolated, with its own collateral, debt, and collateralization ratio. See [Managing Your Trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/managing-your-trove).

Polaris is a <em>factory</em> of CDP instances, each issuing a different pAsset but all sharing the same pETH collateral and bonding curve.

## Keeping the peg

Exchanging a pAsset for its underlying collateral at face value (minus a fee) when it trades <em>below</em> peg. Restores the floor. See [Redemptions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/redemptions).

The inverse of redemption. Arbitrageurs add pETH to troves to mint pAssets when the pAsset trades <em>above</em> peg. Restores the ceiling.

Redemption and minting volume serve as onchain proxy signals for the peg, letting Polaris set rates without an external oracle.

## Risk states

If a trove falls below its minimum collateralization ratio, it is liquidated, and the [Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield/stability-pool) absorbs the debt. See [Liquidations](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/liquidations).

A protocol-wide protective state entered when the system-wide reserve-backing-to-debt ratio (the [TCR](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/resources/glossary)) falls below a fixed threshold. See [Recovery Mode](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/redemptions-liquidations/recovery-mode).

The mathematically enforced minimum price of pETH (in ETH), and that floor as a percentage of market price (your maximum drawdown). See [The Floor Price](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/floor-price).

## Yield & coordination

A reserve of pAssets that processes liquidations and is the primary venue for earning yield. See [The Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield/stability-pool).

The mechanism that mints POLAR by burning pETH; the most protocol-synergetic action possible. See [Conversion Auctions](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/polar/conversion-auctions).

Receives pETH from conversions and bonding-curve swap fees, then buffers and distributes it as yield to troves, pro-rata to their debt. See [The Fee Router](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/fee-router).

A stewardship role steers <em>quantitative parameters within the bounds the contracts define</em>. It does not govern the immutable core. See [Stewardship, not Governance](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/stewardship/stewardship).

## The shape of the system

A single primitive, the bonding curve, produces pETH. pETH collateralizes every pAsset. Activity on any pAsset generates fees; fees burn pETH and raise its floor; a rising floor strengthens every position. POLAR captures the flywheel's long-term value and steers it.

Image: Polaris system overview: ETH swaps into pETH on the bonding curve, pETH collateralizes CDPs that mint pUSD, and burning pETH for POLAR raises the floor and releases ETH

*Key takeaway: one collateral primitive feeds the whole system; pETH creates collateral, CDPs mint pAssets, and POLAR conversion raises the pETH floor.*

The diagram traces four steps and an outcome:

1. **Swap.** ETH enters the **bonding curve** and is issued as **pETH**; the curve captures volatility.
2. **Collateral.** pETH is deposited as the sole collateral for **CDPs**.
3. **Borrow.** A CDP mints **pUSD** (or another pAsset) against that pETH, for scalable onchain yield.
4. **Stewardship.** Burning pETH in a conversion auction mints **POLAR**, raising the pETH floor price and releasing ETH.

> Adoption ⇒ activity ⇒ velocity ⇒ fees ⇒ yield ⇒ more demand.

## Sepolia ETH, test WETH, and pETH

On Public Testnet 1, three "ETH-like" assets can appear in the flow:

- **Sepolia ETH** pays gas on Sepolia. Get it from a public Sepolia faucet; it is not mainnet ETH.
- **Test WETH / reserve token** is the mock reserve token in the Sepolia manifest. The app faucet can mint it when enabled for your wallet. It is not mainnet WETH and has no value.
- **pETH** is the Polaris bonding-curve collateral token. On Public Testnet 1, it is Sepolia-only pETH created from the test reserve token; it is not production pETH.

The first testnet path is: open the [Public Testnet 1 app](https://app.testnet.polarisfinance.io/), switch to Sepolia, mint test WETH/reserve tokens if the faucet is available, swap them through the bonding curve for pETH, borrow or mint a Testnet-active pAsset, then optionally deposit that pAsset into the Stability Pool to test Earn.

## The basic flow

In one line: **deposit ETH ⇒ receive pETH ⇒ open a trove and mint a pAsset ⇒ optionally deposit to the Stability Pool to earn yield.** On Public Testnet 1 this uses Sepolia test assets; in production it uses Ethereum mainnet once launch status says mainnet is live. Step by step:

1. **Deposit or swap into pETH.** On Public Testnet 1, use the app faucet for mock WETH/reserve tokens when available, then use the bonding-curve / swap surface to receive pETH. In production later, this starts from mainnet ETH.
2. **Receive pETH from the [bonding curve](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/peth/bonding-curve).** Your input asset is swapped for pETH at the curve's current price: mock WETH/reserve tokens on Public Testnet 1, and ETH in production later. pETH is an ETH proxy with a floor price that only rises, and the only collateral Polaris accepts.
3. **Open a [trove](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/open-a-trove) and mint a pAsset.** Post your pETH as collateral, then choose which pAsset to mint. pUSD and pGOLD are Testnet-active; pCHF and pBigMac are illustrative or future catalog examples. The pAsset lands in your wallet; the debt stays in your trove. See [the catalog](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/minting/passet-catalog) for what you can mint.
4. **Optionally, earn yield.** Deposit your pAsset into the [Stability Pool](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/yield/stability-pool) to earn a share of borrower interest plus discounted pETH from liquidations. This is optional — you can instead hold, LP, or use your pAsset anywhere in DeFi.

# Why Polaris Finance

## The trilemma

Stablecoins are said to be **scalable, yield-bearing, or trustless — pick two**. Market structure mostly reinforces that framing: the largest stablecoins rely on issuers, custodians, offchain assets, or other counterparties, while cleaner trustless designs stay smaller.

That trade-off is real, but it is not a law of nature. It exists because, for years, high offchain interest rates made it *easy* to back a stablecoin with T-bills and CEX yield and bring that yield onchain. Easy, but not trustless — and not innovative.

## The degradation pattern

The deeper problem is **structural drift toward centralization**. Stablecoins tend to start relatively clean and get dirtier in how they source yield as they grow, because centralized backing is the path of least resistance to scale.

Feature | **USDC** | **USDS / DAI** | **USDe** | **LUSD / BOLD** | **pUSD**

Issuer dependency | Centralized issuer with freeze/blacklist | Governance-set rates, RWA exposure | Market-neutral, custody-dependent | Counterparty-free | Counterparty-free
Yield source | Offchain reserves (not passed to holders) | Mixed: crypto + RWA + governance | Counterparty-dependent infrastructure | Limited onchain sources | Onchain protocol activity
Scalability constraint | Banking relationships | Governance decisions | Counterparty capacity | Capital inefficiency | Bonding-curve scaling
Immutable core | No | Partial | No | Yes | Yes

The lesson: a stablecoin built for trustlessness must be <em>architected</em> so degradation cannot happen, not merely intend to stay clean.

## The counterparty-free lineage

Polaris descends from the line of immutable, counterparty-free CDP stablecoins, built to fix the limitation each prior generation hit.

Image: Polaris lineage: SAI (2017) ⇒ LUSD (2021) ⇒ BOLD / Liquity V2 (2024) ⇒ pUSD (2026)

*The counterparty-free CDP lineage, 2017 to present.*

In text, the lineage reads:

1. **2017 — SAI:** the first CDP stablecoin, single-collateral. It later upgraded to DAI and then drifted toward centralized backing.
2. **2021 — LUSD:** immutable and decentralized, but capital-inefficient, and spent roughly 20% of its life more than 1% above peg.
3. **2024 — BOLD (Liquity V2):** a refined CDP mechanism from the original Liquity developers — still trustless, still yield-constrained.
4. **2026 — pUSD:** the next chapter. A bonding-curve collateral design that solves the overpeg problem and unlocks onchain-scaling yield without adding a counterparty.

## How Polaris resolves it

Polaris is built so the only counterparty you trust is **immutable code and Ethereum**:

- **Onchain-only yield.** Every yield source — borrower interest, bonding curve swap fees, conversion gains, PolarEX swap fees — is internal to the protocol and grows with activity. Nothing is rented from a CEX or T-bill desk.
- **Immutable core.** The bonding curve, CDP logic, liquidations, redemptions, and the Stability Pool are intended to be fixed in audited deployment code. No admin key can add a custodial PSM, external-collateral module, or freeze function under pressure.
- **Counterparty-free by construction.** pUSD sits in the same counterparty-free lineage as LUSD and BOLD — but adds yield that *scales* with DeFi volume rather than capping growth at what a counterparty can deliver.

Having a counterparty means capping your growth to whatever yield that counterparty can deliver.

Polaris turns onchain trading volume itself into a stablecoin yield source through its bonding curve, and extends the model to *any* asset through the [StablecoinOS](https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/core-concepts). DeFi has had uncensorable-but-small and censorable-but-scalable stablecoins. Polaris is built to be the uncensorable, scalable one.
