# Introduction

## Polaris

URL: https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/
Markdown URL: https://tokenbrice.github.io/polaris-docs/index.md

Polaris is a DeFi yield layer built around **pETH**, a yield-bearing collateral asset purposefully designed to back Polaris stablecoins and other synthetic assets.

ETH enters the system through the Polaris bonding curve, pETH becomes the core reserve asset, and protocol activity routes value back into the system. This creates a foundation for onchain money and synthetic assets that does not depend on T-bills, centralized exchanges, custodians, or offchain black-box agreements.

At the center of Polaris are three simple pieces:

- **pETH**, the ETH-backed reserve and collateral asset.
- **pAssets**, decentralized currencies and synthetic assets backed by pETH.
- **POLAR**, the stewardship token that aligns long-term incentives.

Polaris is currently in Public Testnet 1 on Sepolia. Start with the quickstart if you want to try the app, or continue below for the high-level system view.

## The Three Engines

Three tokens work in harmony to sustain Polaris.

Engine | What it is | Why it matters

**pETH** | A yield-bearing reserve asset backed by ETH and powered by the Polaris bonding curve | Provides capital-efficient, yield-bearing collateral with a natively growing floor value
**pAssets** | Decentralized currencies and synthetic assets backed by pETH | Enable scalable onchain money without custodians or external counterparties
**POLAR** | The stewardship token that governs key protocol parameters and captures protocol value | Aligns long-term incentives while keeping the protocol decentralized
